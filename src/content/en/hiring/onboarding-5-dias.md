## DAY 2 — The product and the market (≈5h)

Today you understand what we sell, how money is made from it, and who we compete against. If you're in any client-facing role (sales, support, marketing, finance), this is non-negotiable.

### What a forex/CFD broker is in simple terms

A broker is a platform that connects a retail trader to the financial markets. The client opens an account, deposits money, and trades CFDs (Contracts for Difference) on financial assets: currency pairs (forex), stock indices, metals, crypto, commodities. They don't buy the physical asset; they speculate on its price.

> [!INFO]
> Analogy: the broker is like a regulated casino where the "table" is the global financial market. The client bets on whether a price goes up or down. The house (broker) charges per trade and, in certain cases, takes the other side of the trade.

### How NEOMAAA makes money

Four main sources:

<div class="neo-step-list">
<div class="neo-step" data-num="1"><div><strong>Spread</strong> — difference between buy and sell price. Every time a client opens a trade, they pay the spread. It's the most stable and largest source.</div></div>
<div class="neo-step" data-num="2"><div><strong>Commissions (Raw accounts)</strong> — on Raw accounts the spread is near 0 but we charge a fixed commission per lot traded. Attracts active high-volume traders.</div></div>
<div class="neo-step" data-num="3"><div><strong>Swap (rollover)</strong> — interest charged/paid when the client holds an open position overnight.</div></div>
<div class="neo-step" data-num="4"><div><strong>B-Book flow (partial)</strong> — on clients with a controlled-loss statistical profile, we take the other side of the trade instead of passing it to the market. It's a regulated, industry-standard practice, handled by Pepe under strict policy. More detail on Day 4.</div></div>
</div>

### The 4 account types

| Account | For whom | Spread | Commission | Minimum deposit |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Cent** | Beginners testing with little capital | High | No commission | Very low |
| **Standard** | Typical retail trader | Standard | No commission | Low |
| **Raw** | Active traders / scalpers | Near 0 | Fixed per lot | Medium |
| **Institutional** | Large accounts / managed | Negotiated | Negotiated | High |

> [!TIP]
> As a new agent, **don't recommend an account type without understanding the client first**. Ask: available capital, trading frequency, experience. Recommending the wrong account is the #1 cause of early churn.

### Instruments

More than 2,000 instruments available. Main categories:

- **Forex** — major pairs (EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY), minors and exotics.
- **Indices** — S&P 500, Nasdaq, DAX, Nikkei, etc.
- **Metals** — gold (XAU), silver (XAG), platinum (XPT), palladium (XPD).
- **Stocks CFDs** — individual equities (Apple, Tesla, Amazon, Google, JPMorgan, European companies, etc.) via CFD.
- **ETFs** — exchange-traded funds (SPY, QQQ, sector) via CFD.
- **Crypto CFDs** — Bitcoin, Ethereum and the majors (no real crypto custody, it's a CFD).
- **Commodities** — oil (WTI/Brent), natural gas, agricultural.
- **Energies** — WTI, Brent, NatGas and other energy instruments.

### Platform: MT5 (not MT4)

We operate exclusively on **MetaTrader 5**. Reasons:

- MT4 is legacy. MetaQuotes is pushing everyone to MT5.
- MT5 supports more order types, more timeframes, real market depth.
- Better for multi-asset (stocks, futures besides FX).
- Supports hedging (relevant for our LATAM market).

> [!WARNING]
> If a client asks for MT4, tell them MT5 only. Don't promise we'll add MT4: it's not the direction.

### Why retail traders lose

This is **the most important read of the whole week**. If you don't get this, you don't get the client.

> [!WARNING]
> 70-80% of retail traders lose money. It's a structural industry statistic, not our opinion. Anyone who tells you otherwise is lying to you.

Main reasons:
- **Misused leverage** — the client takes positions too large for their capital.
- **Lack of plan / discipline** — trades on emotion, not system.
- **Cognitive biases** — loss aversion, FOMO, revenge trading.
- **Nonexistent or poor risk management** — no stop loss, no position sizing.
- **Underestimating learning time** — they expect to earn in 3 months what a professional learned in 10 years.

This **matters for your role** because:
- If you are **sales**: don't promise wealth. Build long-term clients with realistic expectations.
- If you are **support**: the client who lost will be emotional. Your job is to listen, contain, not defend.
- If you are **marketing**: any copy promising "guaranteed gains" is illegal and exposes us to sanctions.
- If you are **compliance**: watch for promises being sold that we can't deliver.

## DAY 4 — Compliance, regulation and real risk (≈4h)

Compliance is not a formality. It's what separates a broker that survives from one that closes. Today you understand why every decision we make passes through a regulatory filter.

### Why compliance is not optional

We operate under license. If the license is revoked, the broker closes. If compliance fails:
- Our corporate bank accounts can be frozen.
- The company can be fined amounts that wipe out our cash.
- The principals can be banned from operating in the industry.
- Clients lose trust and leave en masse.

> [!WARNING]
> Compliance is not "the department that says no". It's the department that keeps the business alive. Treat Susana as you would a doctor: don't argue, ask.

### Our regulator: Anjouan (AOFA)

NEOMAAA Markets operates under the **Anjouan Offshore Finance Authority (AOFA)** license, in the Union of Comoros. License L15968/N of Neomaaa Ltd (IBC 15968).

**What it implies:**
- Obligation of rigorous KYC on every client.
- Periodic reports to the regulator.
- Client fund segregation.
- Minimum regulatory capital.
- Applicable international sanctions (OFAC, EU, UN).

**Why Anjouan and not FCA / CySEC:**
- We started with an accessible jurisdiction that allows us to scale fast in LATAM.
- FCA / CySEC are a medium-term target (post-go-live, after generating volume and track record).
- It is not "less serious": it's a newer jurisdiction with more reasonable costs for the launch stage.

### KYC — what we ask from the client

| Document | What for |
|---|---|
| **Passport / ID** | Verify identity |
| **Proof of address** (no more than 3 months old) | Verify residence (jurisdiction) |
| **Selfie with doc** | Liveness check, anti-fraud |
| **Source of funds** (large accounts) | AML, prevent laundering |

Everything is uploaded via **Sumsub**. Review is done by Susana or the compliance team under her supervision.

### AML — alarm signals

> [!WARNING]
> If you see any of these signals, do NOT ignore it. Escalate to Susana immediately.

- Client who deposits and requests a withdrawal without trading (money "laundering" layer).
- Multiple accounts with the same address, IP, or payment method.
- Client who can't explain the source of their funds.
- Documents that appear altered or inconsistent.
- Client classified as PEP (Politically Exposed Person).
- Transactions to/from sanctioned countries.
- Deposit-withdraw pattern without real trading activity.

### Forbidden phrases

> [!WARNING]
> These phrases are ILLEGAL in marketing/sales of financial products. If you say them on a call or write them in a message, you put the company at legal risk.

- "Guaranteed gain"
- "No risk"
- "You'll get rich"
- "I assure you you'll earn X"
- "Zero losses"
- "Foolproof system"
- "Guaranteed capital return"

Correct approach: speak about **probabilities, risk management, conditions, possible outcomes**. Never certainties.

### Restricted countries

We do not accept clients from:
- United States (severe regulation, we have no license)
- North Korea
- Iran
- Syria
- Cuba
- Other sanctioned countries per OFAC / EU / UN (list updates, consult compliance)

If you receive a lead from one of these countries: **reject politely, don't process KYC, escalate to compliance**.

### A-Book vs B-Book — hybrid model

Without going into sensitive detail:

- **A-Book** = we pass the client's order to the market. We earn via spread/commission.
- **B-Book** = we take the other side of the order internally. It's legal, regulated, industry-standard.
- **Hybrid** = we decide per client / per instrument, with criteria defined by Pepe (Head of Dealing) under compliance-approved policy.

> [!INFO]
> If a client asks "are you A-Book or B-Book?", the short answer is: "we operate a regulated hybrid ECN/STP model". Don't go deeper. If they press, escalate to Pepe.
