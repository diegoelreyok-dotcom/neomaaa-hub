## Role Overview

The Marketing Manager is responsible for NEOMAAA Markets' entire marketing strategy and execution. This includes paid advertising, content marketing, social media, SEO, partnerships, and the affiliate program. Their primary goal is to generate qualified client registrations, convert them into deposits (FTDs), and build the broker's brand in the target markets: LATAM (primary), Middle East, Africa, and SE Asia.

---

## Responsibilities

### Strategy and Planning
1. Design and execute the broker's comprehensive marketing strategy, aligned with growth goals at each stage (Stage 1, 2, 3).
2. Define the monthly marketing calendar with campaigns, content, and specific actions.
3. Manage the assigned marketing budget, optimizing the return on every dollar invested.

### Paid Advertising
4. Create, launch, and optimize paid media campaigns on relevant platforms (Google Ads, Meta Ads, TikTok Ads, YouTube Ads, native platforms).
5. Run continuous A/B testing of creatives, copy, audiences, and landing pages.
6. Manage market-specific campaigns (LATAM in Spanish, Middle East in English/Arabic).

### Content and Social Media
7. Develop the content strategy: educational articles, videos, infographics, webinars on forex and trading.
8. Manage the broker's social media accounts (Instagram, Twitter/X, YouTube, TikTok, Telegram, Facebook).
9. Create or supervise daily/weekly content creation across all platforms.

### SEO
10. Implement an SEO strategy for the broker's website: keyword research, on-page optimization, link building, optimized content.
11. Monitor rankings, organic traffic, and improvement opportunities monthly.

### Affiliate Program and Partnerships
12. Design and launch the broker's affiliate / IB (Introducing Brokers) program.
13. Recruit and manage affiliates, trading educators, influencers, and strategic partners.
14. Define commission structure, marketing materials for affiliates, and tracking system.

### Conversion and Funnel
15. Optimize the full funnel: from ad impression to FTD (First Time Deposit).
16. Work with the Sales team to align qualified leads with the sales process.
17. Implement email marketing and automations for lead nurturing.

### Analysis and Reporting
18. Generate weekly and monthly marketing performance reports with key metrics.
19. Analyze data to identify the most profitable channels, highest-value audiences, and optimization opportunities.

## Hire vs. Freelance — Comparative Analysis

This is a pending Principals decision. The analysis below is presented to inform it.

### Option A: Full-Time Marketing Manager ($1,500/month)

**Advantages:**
- Exclusive dedication to the broker, deep product and market knowledge.
- Brand and message coherence across all channels.
- Constant availability to coordinate with Sales, Support, and Principals.
- Institutional knowledge growth — the person accumulates data and learnings.
- Ability to execute long-term strategy with an integrated vision.
- Full control over priorities and delivery times.

**Disadvantages:**
- Fixed monthly cost independent of workload volume.
- At $1,500/month it may be hard to attract senior-level broker marketing talent.
- A single person may not master every discipline (paid media + content + SEO + affiliates).
- If the person doesn't work out, turnover cost is high (retraining, context loss).

### Option B: Specialized Freelancers (variable budget)

**Advantages:**
- Access to specialists by discipline: one paid-media expert, another for SEO, another for content.
- Flexibility to scale up or down based on needs and budget.
- No long-term commitment — providers can be swapped quickly.
- Ability to test multiple approaches in parallel.

**Disadvantages:**
- Requires a Principal (likely Diego) to act as marketing coordinator, consuming executive time.
- Lack of brand coherence without centralized supervision.
- Freelancers have multiple clients — NEOMAAA will not be the priority.
- Total cost can be similar or higher when multiple freelancers are added up.
- Loss of institutional knowledge — each freelancer takes their expertise with them when they leave.
- Slower response times for urgent adjustments.

### Recommendation

**For Stage 1 (launch):** Hire 1 paid-media freelancer focused on LATAM ($500-800/month) + manage content/social in-house. This validates channels and messaging with lower risk.

**For Stage 2 (scaling):** Hire a full-time Marketing Manager to coordinate specialized freelancers and execute the integrated strategy. At that point workload justifies the full-time role.

- Knowledge of the LATAM market: culture, preferred platforms, Latin American trader behavior.
- Native Spanish + professional English.

- Data-driven mindset: decisions based on metrics, not intuition.

### Preferred
- Prior experience in forex broker marketing (not prop trading, not educator — a real broker).
- Experience launching and managing affiliate/IB programs.
- Technical and on-page SEO knowledge.
- Experience with tools: Google Ads, Meta Business Suite, TikTok Ads Manager, Google Analytics, SEMrush/Ahrefs.
- Demonstrable portfolio with past campaigns and their results.
- Basic trading knowledge (can speak credibly with traders).
