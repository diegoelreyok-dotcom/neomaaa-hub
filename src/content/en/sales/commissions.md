## Executive Summary

This document proposes a 3-phase commission structure for NEOMAAA sales agents. The goal is to:

1. **Motivate aggressively at launch** (months 1-3) when there is no client portfolio
2. **Normalize commissions** (months 4-6) once there is a client base generating volume
3. **Stabilize in a sustainable model** (month 7+) aligned with the broker's P&L

- Strong performer: $2,500 - $3,200/month

## 1. Recommended Commission Structure

### Key decision: Fixed commission per FTD vs. percentage of FTD amount

| Criterion | Fixed per FTD ($) | % of FTD amount |
|-----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Simplicity | Very easy to calculate | Requires tracking exact amount |
| Motivation for large FTDs | None - same payout for $50 or $5,000 | Yes - incentivizes pursuing higher capital clients |
| Gaming risk | Lower - no incentive to inflate deposits | Higher - could pressure clients to deposit more |
| Business alignment | Partial - broker earns more from larger clients | High - rewards what matters most to the broker |
| Cost predictability | High - easy to budget | Variable - depends on client mix |

**RECOMMENDATION: Hybrid model**

Use a **base fixed commission per FTD** + a **tiered bonus based on deposit amount**. This combines simplicity with an incentive to pursue higher-value clients.

## 3. Volume Bonus - Detail

### Why it matters

Broker revenue does not come from the FTD itself — it comes from the client actually **trading**. A client who deposits $500 and trades 50 lots generates far more revenue than one who deposits $2,000 and never trades. But to trade, they first need a balance. Re-deposits are the signal that the client is active and trusts the broker.

### Formula

```
Total Monthly Deposits = Sum of ALL deposits from clients assigned to the agent
                        (includes FTDs + re-deposits)

Volume Bonus = Value of the corresponding tier in the active Tier table
```

### What counts as a deposit

- FTDs from new clients (count both for FTD and volume)
- Re-deposits from existing clients
- Deposits via any accepted payment method
- **Does NOT count:** internal transfers between accounts of the same client
- **Does NOT count:** bonuses/credits issued by the broker

### Example - Tier 1

Luis has 15 assigned clients. During the month:
- 6 new clients deposit a total of: $2,800 (FTDs)
- 9 existing clients re-deposit a total of: $4,500

**Total deposits = $2,800 + $4,500 = $7,300**

Per Tier 1 table: $7,000 - $14,999 = **$350 bonus**

## 4. Team Bonus - Detail

### Mechanics

- Calculated at month-end
- Total FTDs of all 3 agents combined are counted
- If the goal is reached, ALL receive the bonus (not just the top producer)
- The highest tier reached applies (not cumulative)

### Why it works

In the broker industry, it's common for agents to "steal" leads from each other or sabotage the team to look better. The team bonus creates incentive to:

1. **Share tactics** that work
2. **Help the teammate** who is behind on quota
3. **Not fight over leads** - if an unassigned lead comes in, whoever can close it takes it, not whoever "saw it first"
4. **Celebrate collective wins** - team culture from day 1

### Example - Tier 1

Monthly results:
- Franco: 18 FTDs
- Edward: 12 FTDs
- Luis: 22 FTDs
- **Team total: 52 FTDs**

Goal reached: 50+ FTDs = **$200 per agent**

## 6. Anti-Gaming Rules

These rules are **non-negotiable** and any violation is grounds for dismissal.

### 6.1 No Fake Accounts
- **Rule:** No commission is paid on accounts belonging to the agent, direct family members (parents, siblings, children, partner), or identifiable close friends.
- **Detection:** Data cross-check: registration IP, KYC data, shared payment methods.
- **Sanction:** First incident = commission clawback + bonus suspension for 1 month. Second incident = dismissal.

### 6.2 No Churning
- **Rule:** Advising a client to withdraw and re-deposit to inflate deposit metrics is not allowed.
- **Detection:** Abnormal deposit/withdrawal ratio (> 3 deposit-withdrawal cycles in 30 days without significant trading activity).
- **Sanction:** Case-by-case review. If confirmed, inflated commissions are clawed back.

### 6.3 No Undue Pressure
- **Rule:** Pressuring a client to deposit more than they can afford is not allowed. This includes suggesting they borrow, use credit cards to deposit, or deposit funds earmarked for essential expenses.
- **Detection:** Client complaints, review of (recorded) conversations, pattern of deposits followed by quick withdrawals.
- **Sanction:** First incident = written warning + mandatory training. Second incident = dismissal.

### 6.4 No FTD Fragmentation
- **Rule:** Splitting an FTD into multiple small deposits to earn more fixed commissions is not allowed. Only the client's first deposit with NEOMAAA counts as FTD, not the first deposit per account type.
- **Clarification:** If a client first opens a Cent account and then a Raw account, only the Cent generates FTD commission. The Raw counts as a re-deposit (goes to the volume bonus).
- **Exception:** If more than 90 days pass between opening the first and second account, it may be considered an independent FTD subject to Diego's approval.

### 6.5 Self-Referrals
- **Rule:** Agents may NOT refer themselves or other agents as clients of the broker.
- **Additional rule:** If an agent trades with a personal account at NEOMAAA (which is allowed), that account is NOT linked to any commission.

### 6.6 Monthly Audits
- Each month, before paying commissions, the Finance Manager (or Diego in Stage 1) reviews:
  - [ ] FTDs with subsequent trading activity (at least 1 executed trade)
  - [ ] No deposit-withdrawal pattern without trading
  - [ ] No duplicate or related accounts
  - [ ] Conversion ratios per agent within normal ranges

## 8. Clawback Policy (Commission Return)

### General Rule

If a client deposits and withdraws without trading, the commission is reversed. This is standard in the industry and protects the broker from ghost clients or fraud.

### Clawback Conditions

| Situation | Clawback triggered? | Return amount |
|-----------|---------------------|---------------|
| Client deposits and withdraws in < 7 days without making any trade | Yes | 100% of FTD commission |
| Client deposits, makes 1-2 minimal trades (< 0.1 lots total), withdraws in < 14 days | Yes | 75% of FTD commission |
| Client deposits, trades normally for 15+ days, then withdraws | No | $0 - commission is final |
| Client deposits, trades, loses entire balance (stop out) | No | $0 - not the agent's fault |
| Client files chargeback | Yes | 100% of FTD commission + deducted from volume bonus |

### Protection Period

- **FTD "maturity" period: 14 days**
- During the first 14 days after the FTD, the commission is "in reserve"
- If the client spends 14 days with active balance and at least 0.5 lots traded, the commission is consolidated
- This does NOT delay payment (commissions pay on day 10), but if a clawback is triggered after payment, it is deducted from the following month

### Clawback Formula

```
Clawback = FTD Commission paid x Percentage per table

Applied against the following month's commissions.
If the agent resigns, it is deducted from their final settlement.
```

### Clawback Cap

- Maximum clawback in a month cannot exceed 30% of the agent's total commissions for that month
- This protects the agent from a situation where multiple clawbacks leave them negative
- Clawbacks exceeding 30% roll over to the following month

### Competitiveness Analysis

**Advantages of our scheme vs. industry:**

1. **Solid base salary ($1,000):** Many LATAM brokers pay $500-$700 base and rely more on commissions. Our base provides stability.
2. **Fast payment (day 10):** The industry typically pays between days 15 and 30 of the following month. Paying on day 10 is an advantage for attracting talent.
3. **Full transparency:** Real-time dashboard. Most brokers have opaque systems where the agent doesn't know what they'll be paid until it arrives.

**Where we fall short:**

1. **Fixed FTD commission is conservative** for Cent/Standard accounts compared to brokers that pay % of FTD (some pay up to 10% of FTD).

**Conclusion:** The scheme is **competitive and balanced** for LATAM. It offers stability (high base) + upside (multiple bonuses) + team culture (team bonus). It's not the highest-paying scheme, but it's the most predictable and fair.

## 10. How Your Variable Compensation Gets Paid — Simplified Mechanics

### Where your commission money comes from

Your variable comes from the revenue the broker generates from the activity of the clients you bring in. In short:

- The broker earns revenue from **spread**, commissions per lot traded, and other income from client trading activity.
- Of that revenue, a **defined portion** goes to compensating sales (your variable). The rest covers operating costs (platform, liquidity, PSPs, support, compliance), regulatory reserves, and company margin.
- Your responsibility is to **bring in real clients who deposit and trade**. The more genuine activity in your portfolio, the higher your variable.

You don't need to know the broker's internal numbers to calculate your commission — your pay depends exclusively on your own performance against the targets defined in this document (FTDs, portfolio volume, retention).

### Why the scheme is sustainable for you

The commission scheme is calibrated to be **profitable for the broker and motivating for you** at the same time. Some key rules:

- **FTD alone is not enough:** if a client deposits and doesn't trade, the FTD commission is clawed back (see section 5). This aligns your incentive with the broker's: we both want active clients, not just deposits.
- **The volume bonus is recurring:** as long as your portfolio trades, you get paid month after month. Keeping clients active is more profitable than burning leads on one-time FTDs.
- **The compensation caps** (up to ~$3,350-$4,000/month depending on tier and performance) are set so top performers earn very well without compromising the model's sustainability.

### What this means in practice

| If you do... | The broker earns... | Your commission... |
|----|----|----|
| FTDs that don't trade | Little or nothing | Gets clawed back (section 5) |
| FTDs that trade consistently | Recurring revenue month after month | You earn FTD + volume bonus + retention |
| Large, active portfolio | Stable revenue base | Scale to Tier 3 with retention bonus (up to $400/month extra) |
| Synthetically inflated volume | Detected in audit | Penalty + potential termination |

### Golden rule

**Your variable is a % of the value you generate — not a gift.** The model is designed so both sides win when you bring in quality clients. When you don't bring in quality clients, both sides lose — that's why clawbacks and minimum KPIs exist (section 12).

If you have doubts about how your variable is calculated in a specific month, the monthly report (sent day 5, paid day 10) details each item per client. Any dispute is resolved 1-on-1 with Diego within 5 business days after.

## 11. Additional Rules and FAQ

### Client Assignment

- Each FTD is assigned to the agent who generated it (recorded in Skale CRM)
- If a lead arrives via marketing (organic, ads) without an assigned agent, it is distributed round-robin
- A client is NEVER reassigned to another agent without Diego's approval
- If an agent resigns, their clients are redistributed among the remaining agents. Volume commissions on those clients apply starting the month following redistribution.

### Broker Bonus Promotions

- If NEOMAAA runs a promotion (e.g., "deposit $500 and receive $100 bonus"), the broker bonus does NOT count as a deposit for commission calculation
- Only real money deposited by the client counts

### Disputes

- Any dispute over commissions is resolved in a 1-on-1 meeting with Diego within 5 business days after the monthly report is published
- Diego's decision is final

### Scheme Review

- This scheme is reviewed every 3 months (at Tier change)
- Any change is communicated with at least 15 days' notice
- Changes are not retroactive (commissions for the current month are paid with the scheme in effect at the start of the month)
