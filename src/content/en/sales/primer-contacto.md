## 1. First Contact Objective

The first contact with a prospect has a single objective: **generate enough trust for the prospect to advance to the next step**.

It's not selling. It's not pressuring. It's not convincing. It is:

1. Understand who the prospect is and what they need
2. Demonstrate that NEOMAAA can serve them
3. Guide them to the next logical step (registration, KYC, or deposit depending on where they are)

**Correct mindset:**
> "I'm here to help this person make an informed decision. If NEOMAAA is for them, great. If not, that's fine too."

**Wrong mindset:**
> "I have to close this sale." — This generates pressure, and pressure generates distrust.

## 2. Lead Sources and Context

### 2.1 Lead Types by Origin

| Origin | Interest Level | Context | Approach |
|--------|---------------|---------|----------|
| **Ads (Meta, Google, TikTok)** | Medium-low | Saw an ad, clicked out of curiosity | Educational, don't push |
| **Web form / Landing** | Medium | Actively searched for a broker | Respond fast, provide solid information |

Before contacting, search in Skale CRM and/or the lead source:

| Data | Where to Find It | Why It Matters |
|------|------------------|----------------|
| Name | Registration form / CRM | Personalize the contact |
| Country | Form / IP | Local payment methods, restrictions |

| Already has an account? | Skale CRM | Don't guide to registration if they already have one |
| KYC status | Sumsub / CRM | Know if verification is done |
| Previous deposits | CRM | Know if they've deposited |

## 4. Discovery Questions

### 4.1 Objective

Discovery questions serve to:
- Understand the prospect's experience level
- Identify which account type to recommend
- Detect potential objections before they arise
- Personalize the value presentation

### 4.2 The 6 Essential Questions

**Question 1 — Experience:**
> "How long have you been trading? Do you already trade with a broker?"

**Why:** Defines whether they're a beginner or experienced. Completely changes the approach.

**Question 2 — Instruments:**
> "What instruments are you most interested in — forex, crypto, indices, stocks?"

**Why:** Allows talking about relevant instruments and verifying we offer them.

**Question 3 — Capital:**
> "What amount would you feel comfortable starting with?"

**Why:** Defines the account type to recommend. Never suggest an amount — let the client say it.

**Question 4 — Platform:**
> "Do you use or have you used MetaTrader 5? Or what platform do you know?"

**Why:** Know whether they need MT5 guidance or are already self-sufficient.

**Question 5 — Objective:**
> "What do you look for in a broker? What's most important to you?"

**Why:** Reveals what value to highlight — whether they care about spreads, security, support, speed, etc.

**Question 6 — Previous objections:**
> "Have you had any bad experience with brokers before?"

**Why:** If yes, you know they need more trust evidence. If no, you can move faster.

### 4.3 How to Use the Answers

| If the prospect says... | Then... |
|-------------------------|---------|
| "I'm new, I've never traded" | Recommend Cent ($5). Educational tone. Offer step-by-step guide. |
| "I have experience, I use IC Markets" | Recommend Raw. Compare conditions. Invite to test in parallel. |
| "I come from NEOMAAA prop" | Already knows the brand. Explain prop vs broker differences. Recommend Standard or Raw. |
| "I'm interested in crypto" | Highlight crypto instruments on MT5 + USDT deposits. |
| "I don't want to risk much" | Cent account, $5. Don't push. "No commitment, no pressure." |
| "I care about spreads" | Present Raw from 0.0 pips. Invite to compare live. |
| "I was scammed before" | Prioritize trust: license, segregated accounts, MT5, testing with little. |
| "I want to deposit $10K+" | Offer Raw or Institutional account. Mention VIP attention. |

## 5. Needs Assessment

### 5.1 Recommendation Matrix

Based on discovery answers:

| Prospect Profile | Recommended Account | Suggested Deposit | Approach |
|------------------|---------------------|-------------------|----------|
| Beginner, low capital | Cent | $5-$50 | Educational, risk-free |
| Beginner, medium capital | Standard | $50-$200 | Educational + MT5 guide |
| Experienced, comparing brokers | Raw | $500-$2,000 | Technical conditions, comparison |
| Professional / high volume trader | Raw or Institutional | $5,000+ | Custom conditions, VIP |
| Comes from NEOMAAA prop trading | Standard or Raw | $50-$500 | Prop vs broker differences |
| Interested in passive income | Standard + Vault/Copy Trading | $200+ | Vault (5% p.a.), Copy Trading |

### 5.2 What Never to Do in Assessment

- **Never suggest a deposit amount greater than what the prospect mentioned.** If they say "$100", don't say "but with $500 you could...". Respect their capacity.
- **Never pressure them to open a higher-tier account.** If they want Cent, Cent is perfect.
- **Never assume their level of knowledge.** Ask before explaining.
- **Never promise trading results.** "Depends on your strategy, not on the account."

## 7. Guide to Registration

### 7.1 When to Guide to Registration

Guide to registration when:
- The prospect asked questions and received satisfying answers
- Showed explicit interest ("how do I register?", "I want to try")
- Has no pending unresolved objections

**Never** guide to registration if:
- The prospect still has fundamental unresolved doubts
- Showed signs of disinterest
- Is from a restricted country

### 7.2 Transition Script to Registration

```
Perfect, [name]. From what you're telling me, the [type] account is
ideal for you.

Registration literally takes 2 minutes. You only need:
- Full name
- Email
- Phone
- Country

I'll send you the link now and if you want I'll stay on the line while
you register in case any question comes up.

[Send registration link]
```

### 7.3 Assistance During Registration

If the prospect registers while in contact:

1. Confirm they received the verification email
2. Guide them to confirm it
3. Immediately after, explain the next step: KYC

## 8. KYC Guide

### 8.1 Transition to KYC

```
Done, your account is created. Now the most important step: verify
your identity. It's a regulatory requirement and helps us protect your
account.

You need two things:
1. A photo of your ID document (national ID, passport,
   or driver's license)
2. A live selfie (the app guides you)

Everything is done from your phone in the Verification section of the
portal. Takes less than 5 minutes. Want me to guide you step by step?
```

### 8.2 Tips for Successful KYC

Share with the prospect:
- "Make sure there's good lighting when you take the document photo"
- "All four corners visible, don't crop anything"
- "For the selfie, look directly at the camera and follow the instructions"
- "If your document is in another language, no problem, our system accepts them"

### 8.3 If KYC is Rejected

```
[name], it looks like the verification needs an adjustment. The reason
was: [reason].

Don't worry, it's common. You just need [corrective action].
You can retry here: [link]

Tips: good lighting, full document, selfie looking straight.
```

## 9. Guide to First Deposit

### 9.1 Correct Timing

Guide to first deposit ONLY when:
- KYC is approved
- The prospect has MT5 credentials
- The prospect has expressed intent to trade

### 9.2 Deposit Guidance Script

```
Your account is verified and ready to trade. Only the deposit remains
to activate it.

Which payment method do you prefer? Based on your country [country], the
fastest options are:
[List 2-3 relevant local methods]

The minimum deposit for your [type] account is $[amount].

Want me to walk you through it?
```

### 9.3 If the Prospect Hesitates to Deposit

Don't push. Use the low-commitment technique:

```
I understand you want to think about it. No rush.

What many traders do is start with the Cent account, which begins at $5.
That way you test the entire process — deposit, trading, and withdrawal —
with a minimal amount before committing more capital.

You decide when and how much. Your account stays active and ready.
```

## 10. Post-Contact Follow-Up

### 10.1 Follow-Up Rules

| Prospect Status | Follow-Up | Channel | Time |
|-----------------|-----------|---------|------|
| Registered but no KYC | KYC reminder | WhatsApp | +6 hours |
| Did KYC but no deposit | Deposit guide | WhatsApp | +12 hours |
| Deposited but no trade | First trade guide | WhatsApp + Intercom | +24 hours |
| Didn't register after contact | Second contact | WhatsApp | +24 hours |
| Didn't reply to first contact | Second attempt | WhatsApp + Email | +48 hours |
| Didn't reply to 3 attempts | Move to email marketing list | Email | +7 days |

### 10.2 Follow-Up Scripts

**24h follow-up (didn't register):**
```
Hi [name], this is [agent] from NEOMAAA. We spoke yesterday and I wanted
to know if you had a chance to check out the platform.

If you have any questions or need help with registration, I'm here.
```

**48h follow-up (second attempt):**
```
Hi [name], just wanted to verify you received my previous message.
If anything changed or you prefer I contact you at another time,
let me know.

If you already decided NEOMAAA isn't for you, I respect that. I just
want to make sure you have all the information to make the decision.
```

**7-day follow-up (last attempt):**
```
Hi [name], this is my last follow-up message. I don't want to be
insistent.

Your NEOMAAA account is still available if in the future you decide
to explore trading. You'll find me here when you want.

I wish you great success.
```

## 11. Errors That Kill Conversion

### 11.1 Top 10 First Contact Errors

| Error | Why It's Serious | Correction |
|-------|------------------|------------|
| Calling without knowing anything about the lead | The prospect feels like just a number | Review CRM before calling |
| Talking more than listening | You don't discover real needs | 60/40 rule: listen 60%, speak 40% |
| Presenting all benefits at once | Overwhelms the prospect | Only present what's relevant to THAT prospect |
| Pushing to deposit | Generates distrust | Guide, don't push |
| Not logging the interaction in CRM | Information is lost, other agents don't know what happened | Log immediately after contact |
| Promising gains or returns | Violates compliance, creates false expectations | Never. "Depends on your strategy and the market" |
| Not having an answer to an objection | Generates insecurity | Study the objections playbook |
| Contacting outside reasonable hours | Annoys the prospect | Respect local time zones |
| Not following up | The prospect cools off | Follow up within 24-48h |
| Speaking ill of the competition | Looks unprofessional | "We respect that broker. I invite you to compare" |
