### Objection 10: "1:1000 leverage sounds dangerous"

**Why they say it:** Has read about leverage risks or doesn't fully understand it.

**Response:**
"You're right to be cautious. First, let's clarify the concept: leverage does not multiply your capital — it reduces the required margin per trade by a factor of 1/N. With 1:500, 1 lot EUR/USD worth ~USD 115,000 notional requires only ~USD 230 margin. But the exposure is still the full notional. Second, that we offer up to 1:1000 doesn't mean you should use the maximum. You choose your account leverage and each trade size. Many experienced traders trade with 1:100 or less. In MT5 you have stop loss, take profit, and you can change leverage when there are no open positions. If you want, we start with conservative leverage and adjust based on your experience."

**Key points:**
- Leverage = margin reducer, not capital multiplier (demystify)
- Leverage is optional, not mandatory
- The client chooses how much to use
- Stop loss and take profit available
- Do not promote high leverage as a main advantage

## Prohibited Phrases (Compliance)

**The sales team CANNOT use the following phrases under any circumstances.**

| Prohibited Phrase | Why it's prohibited |
|---|---|
| "I guarantee you'll make money" | Return promise — violates regulation |
| "It's impossible to lose with this strategy" | False and misleading |
| "Invest everything you can" | Incitement to excessive risk |
| "This is better than a bank" | Inappropriate regulatory comparison |
| "There's no risk" | All investment has risk |
| "Guaranteed gains" | Return promise |
| "Your money is 100% safe" | Can't be guaranteed at 100% |
| "Our Vault is like a term deposit" | Comparison with regulated banking product |
| "You'll recover your investment quickly" | Time-based return promise |
| "Deposit now or miss the opportunity" | Artificial urgency / undue pressure |
| "You don't need experience to win" | Irresponsibly minimizing risk |
| "Tier-1 regulation doesn't matter" | Minimizing regulatory importance |
| "Let me give you investment advice" | We are not regulated financial advisors |
| "This Copy Trading trader always wins" | Past results don't guarantee future |
| "High leverage is an advantage" | Can amplify losses just as much as gains |
| "We are regulated by FCA / CySEC / ASIC / BaFin / CNV / CNMV" (or any tier-1 or national regulator we don't have) | FALSE REGULATORY CLAIM -- we only have Anjouan license L15968/N. Lying about regulation is a crime |
| "Double your money" / "Double your account in X days" / "Triple your capital" | Explicit return promise with a figure |
| "NEOMAAA is better than [competitor]" / "[Competitor] is a scam" / Direct bashing by name | Denigrating comparison -- creates legal risk. Point-by-point comparison is ok, denigration isn't |
| "Signals that never fail" / "Infallible system" / "100% win rate" / "Assured profitability" | Variants of return promise |
| "Borrow money" / "Use your credit card to deposit more" | Inducement to debt |
| "I can speed up your verification" / "Don't worry about KYC, it's just paperwork" | Minimizes compliance or promises improper acceleration |
| "I'll tell you when to buy and sell" / "Let me give you investment advice" | Unlicensed advice |
| "Unlimited leverage" / "$0 minimum deposit" / "Instant fee-free withdrawals" | False offers -- real conditions differ |

## Red Flags — When NOT to Push

**If you detect any of these signs, STOP and escalate to compliance or Edward (Sales Lead).**

### Immediate Escalation:

1. **Client mentions using borrowed or emergency money.** You cannot let someone deposit money they can't afford to lose. Stop and advise on risk.

2. **Client shows signs of trading addiction.** Repetitive losses, frequent deposits to "recover", obsessive language. Escalate to compliance.

3. **Client is from a restricted country (USA, Canada, EEA, UK, Australia, sanctioned).** Stop immediately. Do not continue the registration process under any circumstances.

4. **Client is underage.** Cannot open an account. No exceptions.

5. **Client asks for tax information or financial advice.** We are not financial advisors. Redirect to an independent professional.

6. **Client mentions suspicious activity.** Money laundering, funds of dubious origin, wanting to trade under someone else's identity. Escalate to compliance immediately.

7. **Client becomes aggressive or threatening.** Do not continue the conversation. Document and escalate to Edward.

8. **Client demands return guarantees as a condition to deposit.** You cannot offer guarantees. If they insist, they're not a client we should have.

9. **Client wants to deposit an amount disproportionate to their profile.** If someone who has never traded wants to deposit $50,000, that's a red flag. Escalate to compliance.

10. **Client mentions another "advisor" told them to deposit.** Possible third-party scheme. Verify the client is making their own decisions.

### Escalation Protocol:

1. Thank the client for their interest
2. Indicate you'll consult with a specialist
3. Document the full conversation
4. Send to Edward with a summary of the situation
5. Do not promise resolution or timeline
6. Do not continue selling until you have a response from compliance
