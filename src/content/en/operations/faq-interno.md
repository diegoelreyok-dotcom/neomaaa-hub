## Category 5: KYC and Compliance

### 5.1 What is KYC and why is it necessary?

KYC (Know Your Customer) is the identity verification process required by regulation. It's mandatory for all clients before being able to make withdrawals and, in some cases, before depositing amounts above defined thresholds. NEOMAAA uses Sumsub to automate this process.

### 5.2 What documents do I need to verify?

Two documents are required:
1. **ID document:** Passport, national ID, or driver's license (front and back photo if applicable).
2. **Proof of address:** Utility bill, bank statement, or official document with address (no older than 3 months).

### 5.3 How long does KYC verification take?

With Sumsub, automatic verification can take from 1-3 minutes to a few hours. If manual review is required (blurry documents, non-matching information, minor flags), it can take up to 48 business hours. If EDD applies (Tier 3/4, PEP, large deposit), it can extend to 5 business days. The client receives email notification of the result.

**Client communication rule:** say "24-48 hours" as standard estimated time. Don't promise "minutes" even though most pass automatically.

### 5.4 A document was rejected, what do I do?

Common rejection reasons are: blurry or cropped photo, expired document, illegible information, or proof of address older than 3 months. The client must upload a new document that meets requirements. The agent can check the specific rejection reason in Sumsub.

### 5.5 Can I trade without completing KYC?

**No.** According to official AML/KYC policy, no client can deposit, trade, or withdraw without base KYC approved (ID + PoA + liveness). Additional documentation scales according to the client's risk category defined by Compliance (see [Risk Matrix](/content/compliance/risk-matrix)):

- **LOW RISK**: ID + PoA + liveness (standard KYC)
- **MEDIUM RISK**: + documented Source of Funds (SoF)
- **HIGH RISK**: + Source of Wealth (SoW) + complete EDD + dual approval (Compliance Officer + Director)

The triggers that activate each category are in [EDD Triggers](/content/compliance/edd-triggers). There are no fixed monetary thresholds — categorization is based on client profile, jurisdiction, behavior pattern, and qualitative criteria defined by Compliance Office.

### 5.6 What if I'm a minor?

NEOMAAA Markets only accepts clients over 18 years old. If KYC verification detects that the applicant is a minor, the account will be automatically rejected.

- **Standard:** from 1.0 pips on EURUSD

### 7.2 What commissions do you charge?

Cent and Standard accounts: no commission (cost in spread). Raw account: $3/lot/side ($6 round-turn). Institutional account: negotiable. Gold source: [`encyclopedia/productos-mt5`](/content/encyclopedia/productos-mt5) "Account types" section.

### 7.3 Are there hidden commissions?

No. The only commissions are: spread (included in price), commission per lot on Raw/Institutional accounts, and swap (overnight interest) if positions are held open overnight. No maintenance, inactivity, or account opening fees are charged.

### 7.4 What are swaps?

Swaps are charges or credits for holding positions open overnight (rollover). They're calculated based on the interest rate difference of the currencies of the pair traded. Swaps are applied at 00:00 server time. On Wednesdays, triple swap is applied (to cover the weekend).

## Category 8: Leverage and Margin

### 8.1 What leverage do you offer?

Maximum leverage depends on account type:
- **Cent:** up to 1:1000
- **Standard:** up to 1:500
- **Raw:** up to 1:200
- **Institutional:** up to 1:100

Effective leverage also depends on instrument: forex majors have maximum leverage; exotics, indices, commodities, and crypto may have lower leverage.

**Important conceptual point (Pepe):** leverage **doesn't multiply capital**, it reduces the required margin by 1/N. With 1:500 the margin for 1 lot EURUSD (~USD 115,000 notional) is ~USD 230. The client still assumes all notional exposure; the only thing that changes is how much they tie up in margin. Tier 1 regulators (FCA/ESMA) limit retail leverage to 1:30 on forex major precisely for this reason.

### 8.2 Can I change my leverage?

Yes. The client can request a leverage change from the client portal or by contacting support. The change applies when there are no open positions. Leverage cannot be changed with open positions.

### 8.3 What is a margin call?

A margin call occurs when the account's margin level falls below the defined threshold (generally 100%). It's a warning that the account is approaching stop-out. The client must deposit additional funds or close positions to increase margin level.

### 8.4 At what level is stop-out executed?

Stop-out is executed when margin level falls to 20% (may vary by account type). At that point, positions are automatically closed starting with the one with greater loss, until margin level recovers above threshold. [INTERNAL USE ONLY] Exact margin call and stop-out levels may vary. Verify current configuration with Stanislav if a client reports an unexpected close.

The swap-free account is available for clients who request it. NEOMAAA Markets reserves the right to revoke swap-free status if abuse is detected (use of status for positive swap arbitrage, for example).

## Category 20: Frequent Technical Questions

### 20.1 What is a pip?

A pip (Percentage in Point) is the minimum unit of price movement in forex. For most pairs, a pip is the fourth decimal (0.0001). For pairs with JPY, a pip is the second decimal (0.01). A pip in 1 standard lot of EURUSD equals approximately $10 USD.

### 20.2 What is a lot?

A lot is the unit of measure for trade volume. A standard lot equals 100,000 units of the base currency. There are also mini lots (10,000), micro lots (1,000), and in the Cent account, lots operate in cents.

### 20.3 What is the spread?

The spread is the difference between the buy price (ask) and sell price (bid) of an instrument. It's the main transaction cost in commission-free accounts. A 1.0 pip spread on EURUSD means that when opening a trade, the price must move 1 pip in favor before the position becomes profitable.

### 20.4 What is margin?

Margin is the amount of funds reserved in the account as collateral to maintain an open position. It's calculated according to position size and leverage used. For example, with 1:100 leverage, to open 1 lot of EURUSD ($100,000), $1,000 of margin is required.

### 20.5 What is slippage?

Slippage occurs when an order is executed at a different price from the one requested. It can be positive (better price) or negative (worse price). It's common during high-impact news, market gaps, and low-liquidity hours. NEOMAAA's ECN/STP model minimizes slippage but doesn't eliminate it completely, as it depends on market conditions.
