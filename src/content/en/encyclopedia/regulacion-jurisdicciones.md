## What is a financial regulator

A financial regulator is a public (or quasi-public) authority that **authorizes, supervises and sanctions** entities that handle third-party money: banks, brokers, fund managers, payment platforms.

They exist for three reasons:

1. **Protect the retail client** from fraud, bad practices or bankruptcy.
2. **Protect the financial system** from systemic risk.
3. **Combat money laundering and terrorism financing** (AML/CFT).

For a forex/CFDs broker, the regulator dictates:
- Minimum capital required to operate (from USD 20k offshore to EUR 750k+ in the EU).
- What products it can offer, with what leverage, to which clients.
- How to segregate client funds.
- What information it must publish (risk disclosures, % of losing clients).
- How to handle complaints and disputes.
- Whether a compensation fund exists in case of bankruptcy.

## The 4 regulation tiers

Within the industry, regulators are informally grouped into "tiers" according to how strict they are, how much capital they require, and what client protection they guarantee.

### Tier 1 — Strict regulation

| Regulator | Country | Notes |
|-----------|------|-------|
| FCA | United Kingdom | One of the strictest. FSCS fund up to GBP 85k. |
| CySEC | Cyprus (EU) | MiFID II. European passport. Retail leverage cap 1:30. |
| ASIC | Australia | ESMA-like rules since 2021. |
| FINMA | Switzerland | Few brokers. Very demanding. |
| BaFin | Germany (EU) | Equivalent to CySEC under MiFID. |
| CONSOB | Italy (EU) | Same. |
| AMF | France (EU) | Same. |

**Characteristics:**
- High minimum capital (EUR 730k+).
- Retail leverage cap: 1:30 on majors, 1:20 minors, 1:10 indices, 1:5 stocks, 1:2 crypto.
- Compensation fund (e.g. FSCS UK GBP 85k, ICF Cyprus EUR 20k).
- Mandatory warnings: "X% of retail clients lose money".
- Deposit bonuses prohibited.
- Disputes resolved by public ombudsman.

## How to explain it to the client without lying or getting defensive

The LATAM retail client does NOT understand the 4 regulatory tiers at the same depth as a compliance officer. But they do understand **plain language + concrete data + options**. (If the client is an EEA/UK resident the flow is different: **direct rejection with CR-26 template**, no sales pitch.)

Closing phrases that work:

> "We're an offshore broker with a real license. We chose this jurisdiction because it lets us give you trading conditions that no longer exist in Europe — high leverage, bonuses, multi-currency accounts. Your money is protected by bank segregation, and on retail accounts you have negative balance protection."

> "If your priority is a public fund that covers you like FSCS, UK/CySEC brokers exist. They're more expensive to operate and give you 1:30 leverage. It depends on what you're looking for."

> "Neomaaa Ltd is audited annually by AOFA, has required capital deposited, and any complaint can be escalated to the regulator. License documentation is public on [regulator's site] and on our site."
