## 1. Purpose

This policy sets forth the framework by which **Neomaaa Ltd** (the "Company") identifies, prevents, manages, and discloses **conflicts of interest** that may arise in the course of providing financial intermediation services to its clients.

It is aligned with FATF standards, best practices of regulated brokers (ESMA, FCA, ASIC applicable by analogy), and the requirements of AOFA license L15968/N.

---

## 2. What is a conflict of interest

A conflict of interest exists when the interests of the Company, its employees, or related third parties may **influence, or appear to influence**, the fair, honest, and professional treatment of a client.

In brokerage activity, structural conflicts of interest include:

- **Execution model:** in the internalized portion of flow, the Company acts as counterparty. This creates a **structural conflict of interest** which is managed through (a) quarterly best-execution verification, (b) independent supervision by the Compliance function, and (c) the controls described in Section 4.1 of this document.
- **A-Book component:** the broker charges a markup over the LP spread — creating an incentive to maximize volume, not client outcomes.
- **Bonuses and promotions:** incentive for the client to deposit and trade more.
- **Relationships with IBs/affiliates:** commission paid to intermediaries based on volume creates incentives for over-trading.
- **Yield products (Vault):** Company management of float on client funds.
- **Publication of research or analysis:** the broker or its team may hold positions in the assets discussed.

---

## 3. Guiding principle

Neomaaa Ltd **does not claim to eliminate** all conflicts of interest (structurally impossible in retail brokerage). The commitment is to:

1. **Identify them** proactively.
2. **Manage them** through internal controls (segregation of duties, monitoring, best execution rules).
3. **Disclose them** to the client with transparency.
4. **Treat the client fairly** notwithstanding any structural conflict.

This approach is **industry-standard in retail brokerage**; any broker claiming "zero conflict" is misleading clients.

## 4. Identified conflicts and controls

### 4.1 Hybrid ECN/STP model with partial internalization
- **Conflict:** on the internalized flow, the Company acts as counterparty, which creates a **structural conflict of interest** inherent to the hybrid model.
- **Control:** **best execution** policy (`legal/order-execution-policy.md`). The dealing desk (under the supervision of the Head of Dealing and the Compliance Officer) does not manipulate prices: it ensures that the price received by the client is **comparable to the market price** at the time of execution.
- **Oversight:** Compliance audits executions monthly. The client may request an **execution report** for any trade.

### 4.2 Spread markup on A-Book
- **Conflict:** incentive to increase the markup.
- **Control:** the markup is published on the account specifications page. It cannot be increased unilaterally without prior notice.

### 4.3 Bonuses and promotions
- **Conflict:** incentive for the client to deposit or trade more than is suitable for the client's financial situation.
- **Control:** bonuses carry **clear and documented terms** (`legal/bonus-terms.md`). No sales agent may conceal turnover requirements. Mandatory disclosures in all commercial communications.

### 4.4 IB / affiliate incentives
- **Conflict:** the IB earns on client volume, not on client outcome.
- **Control:** affiliate program with public terms (`legal/affiliate-terms.md`), KYC performed on the IBs themselves, and prohibition on unqualified trading advice by IBs.

### 4.5 Vault Yield System
- **Conflict:** the broker manages the float of the Vault pool and may earn a spread between yield generated and yield paid to the client.
- **Control:** the published yield and product terms are clear (`legal/vault-yield-terms.md`). Wallet segregation between trading flow and Vault pool.

### 4.6 Employees holding personal positions
- **Conflict:** an employee with access to sensitive information could trade in favor of their own positions.
- **Control:** internal **personal trading** policy — employees with access to flow or to execution-related decisions are required to register their personal accounts and may be subject to blackout periods or pre-approval of trades. The specific personal trading policy is defined by Compliance and forms part of employee onboarding.

### 4.7 Outsourcing to vendors with crossed interests
- **Conflict:** liquidity providers, PSPs, or tech vendors may have interests in directing flow or imposing fees.
- **Control:** due diligence on vendors, contracts with best execution and non-discrimination clauses, vendor rotation where bias is detected.

### 4.8 Dealing desk — internal team
- **Conflict:** the dealing desk observes all client flow and could use that knowledge to selectively internalize.
- **Control:** **Chinese wall** with sales/marketing (dealing does not share client information with commercial teams except in formally escalated cases). Routing decision logs are auditable by Compliance.

## 5. Procedure for identifying new conflicts

### 5.1 Responsibilities
- **Every employee** reports to Compliance any situation that may constitute a conflict of interest (new product, new vendor, personal relationship with a client, etc.).
- **The Compliance Officer** maintains the **conflicts register**.
- **Principals** review the register semi-annually.
- **Board / ownership** approves any material conflict before it materializes.

### 5.2 Conflicts register
The register is maintained in the internal Compliance repository defined by policy. It must contain:
- Description of the conflict.
- Parties involved.
- Affected clients (if applicable).
- Controls in place.
- Date of identification, review, and closure.

## 6. Disclosure to the client

The Company **discloses structural conflicts of interest** in:

- **Client Agreement** (`legal/client-agreement.md`) — general framework.
- **Order Execution Policy** (`legal/order-execution-policy.md`) — execution and routing.
- **Website** neomaaa.com — "About" and "Legal Documentation" pages.
- **Marketing materials** — standard disclaimers in any promotional communication.

The client has the right to request **additional information** on conflicts that may affect a specific trade. Contact: `legal@neomaaa.com` or `compliance@neomaaa.com`.

---

## 7. Escalation and resolution

If a client considers that an undisclosed conflict of interest affected a trade:

1. Formal complaint via `legal/complaint-handling.md`.
2. Investigation by Compliance with review of execution logs, communications, and routing decisions.
3. Resolution: if the complaint is legitimate, compensation plus controls adjustment. If not legitimate, a reasoned response is issued to the client.
4. If the client is not satisfied: internal escalation to the Head of Compliance and to the Principals.
5. Final recourse: complaint to the AOFA (regulator).

---

## 8. Internal training

All employees in commercial, dealing, support, and compliance roles receive **mandatory annual training** on conflicts of interest, with examination and record-keeping. New hires: training during onboarding (see `hiring/onboarding-5-dias.md`).
