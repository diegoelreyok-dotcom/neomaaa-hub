## 1. What cookies are

**Cookies** are small text files that a website stores on a visitor's browser. They make it possible to recognize the user, remember preferences, analyze site usage, and personalize the experience. Equivalent technologies include **local storage**, **session storage**, **pixels / web beacons**, **tags**, and mobile **SDKs** — for the purposes of this policy, all such technologies are referred to collectively as "cookies."

## 4. Legal basis

- **Strictly necessary cookies:** legitimate interest of the Company in operating the site.
- **Analytics, functionality, and marketing:** **consent** of the visitor, obtained through the cookie banner on first access.

Consent may be withdrawn at any time from the cookie settings (site footer → "Cookie preferences").

---

## 5. Duration

Cookies may be:
- **Session cookies** — deleted when the browser is closed.
- **Persistent cookies** — remain until their expiration date or until manually deleted. Typical duration: from 30 days to 24 months depending on type.

---

## 6. Consent management

The visitor may:

1. **Accept all cookies** from the initial banner.
2. **Reject all non-essential cookies** and use only strictly necessary ones.
3. **Customize** — accept only certain categories (e.g., analytics yes but marketing no).
4. **Modify the preference at any time** through the "Cookie preferences" link in the footer.

### 6.1 Browser-level settings
The visitor may also delete or block cookies through the browser settings (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge). Note that blocking strictly necessary cookies may impair the functioning of the site (for example, logging in).

### 6.2 Do Not Track (DNT)
The Company honors DNT signals where technically feasible. However, no industry-wide uniform standard exists for interpreting the signal.
