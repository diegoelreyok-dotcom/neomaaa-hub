## 1. INTRODUCTION

Neomaaa Ltd (IBC 15968), holder of International Brokerage License Number L15968/N issued by the Anjouan Offshore Finance Authority (AOFA), is committed to the fair, transparent, and timely handling of client complaints.

This Policy sets forth the formal procedure for the submission, handling, and resolution of complaints.

---

## 2. DEFINITION OF COMPLAINT

A complaint is any formal expression of dissatisfaction from a client regarding the services provided by the Company, including but not limited to:

- Order execution (slippage, rejection, delay).
- Deposits and withdrawals (delays, rejections, incorrect amounts).
- KYC verification (delays, rejections, excessive requirements).
- Trading conditions (spreads, swaps, leverage).
- Client service (lack of response, inadequate treatment).
- Account security (unauthorized access, unsolicited changes).
- Platform errors (technical failures, price errors).
- Marketing communications (misleading or incorrect information).
- Improper charges (undisclosed commissions, incorrect charges).

---

## 3. CHANNELS FOR SUBMITTING A COMPLAINT

The client may submit a formal complaint through the following channels:

| Channel | How to Use |
|-------|----------------|
| **Email (complaints)** | `support@neomaaa.com` with subject line "Formal Complaint - [Account Number]". |
| **Email (legal)** | `legal@neomaaa.com` — official legal email published on neomaaa.com. For formal legal complaints, subpoenas, and contractual disputes. |
| **Email (compliance)** | `compliance@neomaaa.com` — for AML/KYC, PEP, and sanctions matters. |
| **Live chat (Intercom)** | Initiate a conversation and request that it be logged as a formal complaint. The support agent will escalate to the relevant department. |
| **Client Portal** | Use the complaint form available in the support section of the Client Portal. |

### 3.1 Required Information

To process a complaint efficiently, the client must provide:

1. Full name and trading account number.
2. Detailed description of the issue or complaint.
3. Relevant dates and times (particularly for execution matters).
4. Ticket or order numbers involved (if applicable).
5. Supporting evidence (screenshots, prior emails, etc.).
6. Resolution sought by the client.

---

## 4. COMPLAINT HANDLING PROCESS

### 4.1 Stage 1 — Acknowledgment of Receipt

- The Company will acknowledge receipt of the complaint within **24 business hours** of receipt.
- A unique reference number will be assigned to the complaint.
- The client will be informed of the estimated timeline for resolution.

### 4.2 Stage 2 — Investigation

- The responsible department (Support, Compliance, Dealing Desk, Operations) will investigate the complaint.
- Relevant records will be reviewed (server logs, trade history, communications, transaction records).
- If additional information is required from the client, it will be requested clearly and specifically.

### 4.3 Stage 3 — Resolution

- The Company will provide a formal response to the client within the established timelines (Section 5).
- The response will include:
  - A summary of the facts investigated.
  - The outcome of the investigation.
  - The action taken (if applicable).
  - Justification of the decision.

### 4.4 Stage 4 — Appeal

If the client is not satisfied with the resolution:

- The client may request a second-level review within 10 business days of receipt of the response.
- The review will be conducted by a senior member of the Compliance team who did not participate in the original investigation.
- The second-level decision will be communicated within an additional 10 business days.

---

## 5. RESOLUTION TIMELINES

| Type of Complaint | Resolution Timeline |
|---------------|-------------------|
| **Simple complaints** (information, communication, access) | 5 business days |
| **Standard complaints** (deposits, withdrawals, verification) | 10 business days |
| **Complex complaints** (execution, price disputes, technical errors) | 20 business days |
| **Second-level review (appeal)** | Additional 10 business days |

If the Company anticipates that resolution will exceed the established timelines, it will notify the client with an explanation and a new estimated timeline.

---

## 6. COMPENSATION

6.1 Where the investigation determines that the Company committed an error or failure resulting in harm to the client, the Company may offer:

- Correction of the affected transaction (price adjustment, reopening of position).
- Refund of commissions or spreads improperly charged.
- Credit to the client's account.
- Any other form of compensation deemed fair and reasonable.

6.2 Compensation will not include losses resulting from market movements occurring after the event giving rise to the complaint.

6.3 All compensation requires the approval of the Compliance department.

---

## 7. COPY TRADING COMPLAINTS

7.1 The Company is not responsible for the trading decisions of signal providers in the Copy Trading service.

7.2 Complaints concerning the performance of signal providers do not constitute valid complaints against the Company, except where they involve a technical failure of the platform.

7.3 Complaints concerning the technical functionality of Copy Trading (replication errors, execution delays) are processed in accordance with this Policy.

---

## 8. RECORDING AND REPORTING

8.1 All complaints are recorded in a centralized system that includes:

- Reference number.
- Date of receipt and date of resolution.
- Responsible department.
- Summary of the complaint and resolution.
- Status (open, under investigation, resolved, appealed).

8.2 The Company maintains complaint statistics including:

- Total number of complaints per period.
- Average resolution time.
- Most frequent categories.
- Percentage resolved in favor of the client.

8.3 These records are available for AOFA review in the event of an audit.

---

## 9. ESCALATION TO AOFA

9.1 If the client is not satisfied with the resolution provided by the Company after exhausting the appeal procedure, the client has the right to escalate the complaint to the Anjouan Offshore Finance Authority (AOFA).

9.2 AOFA contact information and the escalation procedure are available on the official AOFA website.

9.3 The Company will fully cooperate with any investigation initiated by AOFA.

---

## 10. CONFIDENTIALITY

All information relating to complaints is treated as confidential. Only authorized personnel have access to complaint records, and information is shared with third parties only where required by law or regulation.

---

## 11. COMPANY COMMITMENT

The Company commits to:

- Handle all complaints fairly, impartially, and promptly.
- Not discriminate or retaliate against clients who submit complaints.
- Use complaints as an opportunity to improve its processes and services.
- Train its personnel in the proper handling of complaints.
