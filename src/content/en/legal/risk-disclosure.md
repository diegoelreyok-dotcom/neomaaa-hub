## 2. GENERAL TRADING RISKS

### 2.1 Risk of Capital Loss

Trading CFDs and other derivative financial instruments involves a high level of risk. The vast majority of retail investor accounts lose money when trading CFDs. You may lose all of your invested capital. You should not deposit funds that you cannot afford to lose without this affecting your financial position or standard of living.

### 2.2 Leverage Risk

NEOMAAA offers leverage of up to 1:1000 on certain account types and instruments. Leverage allows trading positions significantly larger than the capital deposited. While this can amplify potential gains, it also amplifies losses in the same proportion. A leveraged position may generate losses that exceed the initial deposit.

### 2.3 Market Risk

The prices of financial instruments fluctuate due to multiple factors, including:

- Macroeconomic conditions and central bank decisions
- Geopolitical events and natural disasters
- Market sentiment and capital flows
- Publication of economic data and indicators
- Regulatory changes in various jurisdictions

Such price movements are unpredictable and may occur suddenly and significantly, resulting in substantial losses.

### 2.4 Volatility Risk

Financial instruments may experience periods of high volatility, during which prices move rapidly and unpredictably. Volatility may be particularly pronounced during:

- Release of high-impact economic news
- Market session opens and closes
- Periods of low liquidity (holidays, weekends)
- Financial crises or unforeseen events (black swans)

### 2.5 Liquidity Risk

Under certain market conditions, it may be difficult or impossible to liquidate a position at the desired price. This may occur during periods of low liquidity, high-volatility events, or when trading instruments with lower market depth.

### 2.6 Gap Risk (Price Gaps)

Prices may experience gaps between the closing price and the opening price, particularly over weekends, holidays, or following significant news events. Stop loss orders do not guarantee execution at the specified price; they may be executed at the next available price, which may differ significantly.

## 3. INSTRUMENT-SPECIFIC RISKS

### 3.1 Forex (Currency Pairs)

Currency pairs are sensitive to monetary policy, interest rate differentials, trade balances, and geopolitical factors. Exotic pairs present lower liquidity and wider spreads.

### 3.2 Cryptocurrency CFDs

Cryptocurrencies present extremely high volatility. Prices may fluctuate 10% or more in a single day. They are subject to additional regulatory risks, as jurisdictions may prohibit or restrict trading at any time.

### 3.3 Equity and Index CFDs

Subject to specific corporate risks (quarterly results, mergers, bankruptcies) and general stock market volatility.

### 3.4 Precious Metals and Energy CFDs

Commodity prices are influenced by supply and demand factors, OPEC decisions, geopolitical conflicts, and weather conditions.

### 3.5 ETF and Futures CFDs

These instruments have additional features such as expiration dates, rollovers, and associated costs which the Client must understand before trading.

## 4. OPERATIONAL AND TECHNOLOGICAL RISKS

### 4.1 Execution Risk

Orders are executed at the price available at the time of execution, which may differ from the requested price (slippage). Slippage may be positive or negative and is more likely during periods of high volatility or low liquidity.

### 4.2 Technology Risk

Trading through electronic platforms such as MetaTrader 5 is subject to technological risks, including:

- Failures in the Client's internet connection
- Interruptions on trading servers
- Software errors or platform updates
- Cyberattacks or infrastructure failures

NEOMAAA will not be liable for losses arising from technological failures outside its reasonable control.

### 4.3 Margin Call and Stop Out Risk

When the margin level of an account falls below the established thresholds, the system may issue a Margin Call or execute an automatic Stop Out, closing open positions in order to protect the account from a negative balance. The Client is responsible for monitoring the margin level at all times.

## 5. ADDITIONAL FINANCIAL RISKS

### 5.1 Trading Costs

Trades are subject to costs that include spreads, commissions (on Raw and Institutional accounts), and swaps on positions held overnight. These costs reduce potential profitability and accumulate with each trade.

### 5.2 Exchange Rate Risk

If the Client trades in a currency different from the account's base currency, applicable currency conversions may affect the outcome of trades.

### 5.3 Counterparty Risk

When trading CFDs, the Client enters into a contract with NEOMAAA as counterparty or with NEOMAAA's liquidity providers. This entails inherent counterparty risk.
