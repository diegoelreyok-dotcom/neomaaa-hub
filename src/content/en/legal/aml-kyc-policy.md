## 1. INTRODUCTION

Neomaaa Ltd (IBC 15968), holder of International Brokerage License Number L15968/N issued by the Anjouan Offshore Finance Authority (AOFA), is committed to the prevention of money laundering (Anti-Money Laundering, AML) and the financing of terrorism (Counter-Financing of Terrorism, CFT).

This Policy describes the measures the Company implements to comply with its regulatory obligations and to safeguard the integrity of its operations.

## 2. REGULATORY FRAMEWORK

The Company operates in accordance with:

- The AML/CFT regulations established by AOFA.
- The recommendations of the Financial Action Task Force (FATF).
- The sanctions resolutions of the United Nations Security Council.
- Applicable sanctions lists (OFAC, EU, UN).

## 3. KNOW YOUR CUSTOMER (KYC) POLICY

### 3.1 Identity Verification

All clients must complete an identity verification process prior to depositing, trading, or withdrawing funds. The process includes:

**Required documents:**

| Document | Purpose | Requirements |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| **Identity document** | Verify the client's identity | Valid passport, national ID card, or driver's license. Must include full name, date of birth, photograph, and expiration date. |
| **Proof of address** | Verify residential address | Utility bill, bank statement, or official document. No older than 3 months. |
| **Biometric verification** | Prevent identity fraud | Selfie or live verification (liveness check) through the verification platform. |

### 3.2 Client Risk Classification

Neomaaa Ltd classifies its clients into three risk categories (LOW, MEDIUM, HIGH) in accordance with its internal risk matrix. The specific criteria and triggers are operational property of the Compliance Office and are documented in the internal policy (AML/KYC Risk Matrix), available to auditors upon request.

| Category | Risk Profile | Documentation Required |
|-----------|------------------|-------------------------|
| **LOW RISK** | Standard retail client, non-restricted jurisdiction, profile consistent with declaration | ID + Proof of address + Biometric verification |
| **MEDIUM RISK** | Qualitative triggers defined in the internal Risk Matrix | LOW RISK + Source of Funds declaration and supporting documentation |
| **HIGH RISK** | PEP, sanctions list match, FATF high-risk jurisdiction, opaque structures, or other categorical triggers | MEDIUM RISK + full EDD (see Section 4) + dual approval by Compliance and Principals |

### 3.3 Verification Provider

The Company uses Sumsub as its KYC verification services provider, including:

- Automated document recognition.
- Biometric verification (liveness check).
- Automated screening against international sanctions lists.
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEP) screening.
- Adverse media screening.

### 3.4 Verification Timelines

- Automated verification is completed within minutes in most cases.
- Manual reviews may take 48 to 72 business hours, depending on the risk category and the quality of the documentation provided.
- Where documentation is insufficient, the client will receive instructions to correct and resubmit.

## 4. ENHANCED DUE DILIGENCE (EDD)

The Company applies Enhanced Due Diligence procedures in line with FATF Recommendation 12 for clients categorized as HIGH RISK. Categorical triggers include:

- PEP status (personal, family member, or close associate of a Politically Exposed Person).
- Match against international sanctions lists (OFAC, UN, EU).
- Jurisdictions included in the FATF grey or black list (high-risk jurisdictions).
- Opaque corporate structures or structures where the ultimate beneficial owner cannot be identified.
- Risk-related behavioral patterns (transactions inconsistent with the client's profile, structuring).
- Any circumstance resulting in an elevated risk level under the internal Risk Matrix.

EDD may include requests for additional documentation, interviews, independent validation of sources, and temporary restrictions on the account until the review is completed.

## 5. TRANSACTION MONITORING

### 5.1 Ongoing Monitoring

In accordance with FATF Recommendation 10, the Company maintains ongoing monitoring of all client accounts and transactions to detect unusual or potentially suspicious activity, including:

- Deposits or withdrawals of unusually high amounts.
- Deposits followed by immediate withdrawals without significant trading activity.
- Use of multiple payment methods without apparent justification.
- Transactions inconsistent with the client's declared financial profile.
- Structuring attempts (splitting transactions to avoid reporting thresholds).

**Periodic reviews by risk category:**

| Category | Review Frequency |
|-----------|---------------------|
| HIGH RISK | Monthly |
| MEDIUM RISK | Quarterly |
| LOW RISK | Annual or upon a triggered event |

### 5.2 Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs)

Where the Company identifies suspicious activity, it will submit a Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) to AOFA in accordance with applicable regulation. The Company will not inform the client that a SAR has been filed.

---

## 6. PROHIBITION OF THIRD-PARTY DEPOSITS

6.1 All deposits must originate from accounts or payment methods held in the name of the trading account holder.

6.2 Third-party deposits are not accepted, except in exceptional circumstances duly documented and approved by the Compliance department.

6.3 Funds received from third parties without prior authorization will be returned to the sender.

---

## 7. RESTRICTED COUNTRIES

The Company does not accept clients from the following jurisdictions:

**Internationally sanctioned (FATF black list / OFAC / UN / EU / UK — automatic rejection):** Cuba, Iran, Iraq, Myanmar (Burma), North Korea (DPRK), Sudan, Syria, Crimea, the Donetsk and Luhansk regions, and any individual or country under specific sanctions.

**Regulatory restrictions (internal or local regulatory policy):** the United States of America, Canada, the European Economic Area (30 countries), the United Kingdom, Australia, Japan, Israel.

**Enhanced monitoring (FATF grey list):** Clients resident in FATF grey-listed jurisdictions are automatically classified as MEDIUM RISK and must provide mandatory Source of Funds documentation. The current list is maintained in `compliance/risk-matrix.md` Section 5 and `compliance/screening-sanciones.md` (gold source).

Clients attempting to register from restricted countries will be automatically rejected during the verification process.

---

## 8. INTERNATIONAL SANCTIONS

8.1 The Company performs automated screening of all clients against international sanctions lists (OFAC, UN, EU) during the verification process and on a periodic basis.

8.2 No accounts will be opened and no transactions will be processed for persons or entities included on sanctions lists.

8.3 If an existing client becomes sanctioned, their account will be frozen immediately and the competent authorities will be notified.

---

## 9. RECORD KEEPING

The Company retains all verification, transaction, and communication records for a minimum period of 5 years following account closure or the last transaction, in accordance with AOFA requirements.

---

## 10. STAFF TRAINING

All Company personnel receive periodic AML/CFT training, including:

- Identification of suspicious activity.
- Escalation procedures.
- Prohibited phrases in client communications.
- Regulatory updates.

---

## 11. COMPLIANCE OFFICER

The Company designates a Compliance Officer responsible for:

- Overseeing compliance with this Policy.
- Reviewing and approving high-risk verifications.
- Filing SARs with AOFA.
- Coordinating with regulatory authorities.
- Leading the team's AML/CFT training.

---

## 12. COOPERATION WITH AUTHORITIES

The Company will fully cooperate with legitimate information requests from regulatory, judicial, or law enforcement authorities, in accordance with applicable law.
