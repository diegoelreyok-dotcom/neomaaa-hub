## 1. Fundamental Principles

All communication from NEOMAAA Markets with clients is based on five principles. These are non-negotiable regardless of channel, language, or situation.

### 1.1 The Five Principles

| Principle | Meaning | Example |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| **Clarity** | The client understands exactly what we say, without ambiguity | "Your withdrawal will be processed within 1-3 business days" instead of "it will be processed soon" |
| **Empathy** | We acknowledge what the client feels before offering solutions | "I understand your frustration with the delay" before explaining the process |
| **Honesty** | We never promise what we can't deliver, we never hide information | "I can't give you an exact time, but I'll follow up every 4 hours" |
| **Proactivity** | We anticipate the next question and answer it before it's asked | "Your deposit is being processed. In the meantime, you can already download MT5 with these instructions..." |
| **Professionalism** | We maintain composure and respect in every circumstance, even under pressure | We never respond with sarcasm, defensiveness, or indifference |

### 1.2 The Golden Rule

> Every person who contacts us is trusting us with their money. That trust is earned with every interaction and lost with a single negative experience. Treat every conversation as if it were the client's first impression of NEOMAAA.

### 1.3 Brand Identity in Communication

NEOMAAA is not a cold corporate broker nor an aggressive salesperson. We are:

- **Accessible**: We speak like people, not like robots or a legal manual
- **Competent**: We know our product and give precise answers
- **Trustworthy**: We keep what we promise, always
- **Direct**: We don't beat around the bush, we get to the point respectfully

## 2. Tone by Communication Channel

The base tone is the same, but adapts to the channel.

### 2.1 Intercom (Live Chat)

**Tone:** Conversational but professional. Quick replies, short paragraphs.

**Characteristics:**
- Personalized greeting with client's name
- Maximum 3-4 lines per message before taking a pause
- Use of numbered lists for instructions
- Confirm understanding: "Is this clear to you?" or "Do you need me to explain anything else?"
- Closing with open offer for help

**Correct example:**
```
Hi John, this is Rocio from NEOMAAA.

I see that your $200 deposit via PIX was registered 45 minutes ago. 
I'm verifying it with the payments team right now.

I'll confirm the status within the next 15 minutes. In the meantime, 
is there anything else I can help you with?
```

**Incorrect example:**
```
Hello, your deposit is in process, please wait. Deposits take between 1 and 24 hours. If you have more questions visit our FAQ section.
```

### 2.2 WhatsApp

**Tone:** Friendly and direct. More informal than Intercom but equally professional.

**Characteristics:**
- Shorter messages (1-3 lines)
- Use of line breaks for readability
- You can use informal address if the client does first
- Don't send multiple messages in a row without waiting for a response
- Always identify yourself: "This is [name] from NEOMAAA"

### 2.3 Email

**Tone:** Formal but warm. Full structure with descriptive subject.

**Characteristics:**
- Subject that describes the action or status (not generic)
- Full greeting
- Body organized into sections if long
- Closing with name, title, and contact details
- Include risk disclaimer when discussing trading

**Signature format:**
```
[Name]
[Title] — NEOMAAA Markets
Support: Intercom at neomaaa.com
Email: [email protected]

Trading forex and CFDs carries risk of loss. Only trade with capital you can afford to lose.
```

### 2.4 Phone

**Tone:** Professional, warm, unhurried. No rush.

**Characteristics:**
- Full introduction at the start: "Hi [name], this is [agent] from NEOMAAA Markets"
- Listen completely before responding
- Summarize what the client said to confirm understanding
- Close with a summary of next steps
- Send confirmation email after the call with what was agreed

## 3. Approved and Prohibited Vocabulary

### 3.1 Approved Words and Phrases

| Situation | Use | Example |
|-----------|------|---------|
| Acknowledge a complaint | "I understand", "I get it", "That's valid" | "I understand your concern about the withdrawal time" |
| Offer help | "Let me check", "I'll investigate", "I'll help you with that" | "Let me check the status of your transaction" |
| Follow up | "I'll confirm in [time]", "I'll keep you informed" | "I'll confirm the status within the next 30 minutes" |
| Explain a process | "The process works like this", "What happens is" | "What happens is that wire withdrawals require 1-3 business days" |
| Admit a mistake | "We made a mistake", "This shouldn't have happened" | "You're right, this shouldn't have taken this long. Let me fix it" |
| Deliver bad news | "I'm sorry to inform you that", "Unfortunately" | "I'm sorry to inform you that the payment method isn't available in your region" |
| Close positively | "I'm here if you need anything else", "Don't hesitate to contact us" | "Any other questions, I'm here to help" |

### 3.2 Prohibited Words and Phrases

| Don't use | Reason | Alternative |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| "It's not my area" | Sounds like evasion | "Let me transfer you to the specialist who can help you better with this" |
| "I can't do anything" | Sounds like indifference | "I'll escalate your case to the specialized team to find a solution" |
| "That's what the policy says" | Sounds rigid and cold | "I understand your point. The reason for this process is [explain why]" |
| "You should have..." | Sounds accusatory | "For next time, I recommend [action]. Now let's resolve the current situation" |
| "Obviously" / "It's logical" | Sounds condescending | Explain without assuming it's evident |
| "I don't know" (without follow-up) | Sounds incompetent | "I don't have that information right now, but I'll verify and respond in [time]" |
| "Calm down" / "Take it easy" | Invalidates the client's emotions | "I understand this situation is frustrating. Let's resolve it together" |
| "It's your fault" | Unacceptable | "What may have happened is [explain situation]. Let's see how to solve it" |
| "Always" / "Never" | Absolutes that generate confrontation | Use "generally", "in most cases" |
| "As I explained before" | Sounds condescending | Repeat the explanation patiently, perhaps with a different approach |

### 3.3 Language Rules

- **Formal or informal address:** Follow the client's cue. If the client uses informal address, you can too. If formal, use formal. When in doubt, start formal and adapt.
- **Technical jargon:** Use only if the client demonstrates familiarity. If not, explain in simple terms.
- **Acronyms:** Always spell out the first time: "MetaTrader 5 (MT5)", "Know Your Customer (KYC)".
- **Numbers and currencies:** Always specify currency: "$500 USD", not "$500". Use comma decimal format for LATAM when necessary.

## 4. Tone by Situation

### 4.1 New Client / First Contact

**Goal:** Make them feel welcome and in good hands.

**Tone:** Warm, enthusiastic (without exaggerating), guidance-oriented.

**What to do:**
- Welcome by name
- Ask what they need before assuming
- Offer proactive guidance for the next step
- Don't overwhelm with information they didn't ask for

**Base script:**
```
Hi [name], welcome to NEOMAAA. I'm [agent] and I'm here to 
help you with whatever you need.

Tell me, do you already have your account created or are you 
in the registration process?
```

### 4.2 Satisfied Client / Actively Trading

**Goal:** Reinforce the relationship, offer additional value.

**Tone:** Friendly, professional, with focus on optimizing their experience.

**What to do:**
- Resolve their inquiry quickly and efficiently
- Mention features they might not know (Copy Trading, Vault, VPS)
- Thank them for their loyalty if they've been with us for a while

**Base script:**
```
Hi [name], thanks for contacting us. [Resolve inquiry].

By the way, do you know our [Vault/Copy Trading/VPS] feature? 
It can complement your trading style well. If you're interested, 
I'll send you the information.
```

### 4.3 Frustrated / Upset Client

**Goal:** De-escalate the emotion, solve the problem.

**Tone:** Calm, empathetic, solution-focused. Zero defensiveness.

**LEAR Protocol (Listen, Empathize, Act, Resolve):**

1. **Listen:** Let the client express their full frustration without interrupting.
2. **Empathize:** Acknowledge what they feel. "I completely understand your frustration."
3. **Act:** Explain what you'll do to resolve it. "Here's what I'll do right now..."
4. **Resolve:** Execute the solution and confirm the client is satisfied.

**Base script:**
```
I understand your frustration, [name]. This isn't the experience we want 
to give you and I'm sorry it happened.

Let me review your case right now. [Describe concrete action you'll 
take]. I'll confirm the result in [specific time].

I want to make sure this gets resolved properly.
```

**What never to do with a frustrated client:**
- Don't ask them to "calm down"
- Don't minimize the problem ("it's not that bad", "that happens all the time")
- Don't blame the client
- Don't give generic excuses
- Don't transfer from person to person without prior summary

### 4.4 Client Who Lost Money Trading

**Goal:** Provide emotional support without assuming responsibility for trading decisions.

**Tone:** Understanding, honest, non-paternalistic. This is one of the most delicate moments.

**Key principles:**
- Trading carries risk, but never say "you knew this" or "that's normal"
- Don't offer trading advice or suggest how to recover losses
- Don't minimize the loss ("it was little money", "we all lose sometimes")
- If the client shows signs of extreme distress or talks about money needed to live, escalate to compliance

**Base script:**
```
I'm sorry your trades didn't turn out as expected, [name]. 
Trading has tough moments and I understand how frustrating it can be.

I want to confirm that everything worked correctly on the platform. 
Did your orders execute at the conditions you set?

If there's anything technical you'd like us to review (execution, 
spreads, slippage), we'd be happy to check. And if you prefer, you 
can adjust your leverage or your position sizes from MT5.
```

**If the client asks for advice on what to trade:**
```
I understand you want guidance, but as an execution broker, we 
can't provide investment recommendations or financial advice.

What I can do is show you how our Copy Trading works, where 
you can follow traders with a verified track record. And we 
always recommend trading only with capital you can afford to lose.
```

**Warning signs — escalate to compliance:**
- "I need to recover that money, it's for rent"
- "I'm going to deposit more to recover the losses"
- "They told me with this strategy I would win for sure"
- "I'm desperate"

### 4.5 VIP / High-Value Client

**Goal:** Make them feel they receive differentiated treatment.

**Tone:** Highly professional, personalized, proactive.

(See full document: `support/atencion-vip.md`)

**Base script:**
```
Hi [name], this is [agent], your dedicated contact at NEOMAAA.

I've reviewed your inquiry and [action taken]. I want to make sure 
everything is resolved to your satisfaction.

If you need anything additional, you have my direct line. I'm 
available to you during [hours].
```

### 4.6 Client from Restricted Country

**Goal:** Inform respectfully that we can't serve them. Without leaving a bad impression.

**Tone:** Respectful, clear, unambiguous. Leave no room for interpretation.

**Base script:**
```
Hi [name], thanks for your interest in NEOMAAA Markets.

Unfortunately, for regulatory reasons, we cannot open accounts or 
offer services to residents of [country]. This restriction applies 
to all residents of that jurisdiction, without exceptions.

We're sorry we can't help you on this occasion. We wish you success 
in your trading.
```

**What never to say:**
- "You can use a VPN"
- "If you have someone in another country..."
- "Maybe this will change later"

### 4.7 Client Threatening Social Media / Negative Review

**Goal:** Don't react with fear or aggression. Resolve professionally.

**Tone:** Calm, solution-focused, without yielding to pressure.

**Base script:**
```
[name], my priority is resolving your case correctly.

I understand your frustration and I want to make sure you get 
the attention you deserve. Let me [concrete action] and I'll confirm 
the result in [time].

Our commitment is to resolve this through the proper channels.
```

**What never to do:**
- Don't threaten back
- Don't promise things outside of policy out of fear of the review
- Don't ignore the case hoping it will be forgotten
- Document all interaction and escalate to Edward if persists

## 5. Emotional Intelligence in Trading

### 5.1 Understand the Emotional Context

Broker clients experience intense emotions because their money is at stake. Unlike other services, here the emotional impact is immediate and personal.

| Client Moment | Likely Emotion | How to Respond |
|-------------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Just registered | Curiosity, enthusiasm, some distrust | Guide with confidence, don't push |
| First deposit | Nervousness, hope | Reassure, confirm quickly |
| Won their first trade | Euphoria, excessive confidence | Congratulate briefly, don't feed overconfidence |
| Lost money | Frustration, anger, sadness, regret | Empathize, don't minimize, don't advise |
| Delayed withdrawal | Anxiety, distrust | Inform proactively, give clear times |
| Technical issue | Stress, urgency | Resolve fast, offer alternatives |
| Wants to close account | Disappointment, resolve | Don't beg, ask reason, offer alternative if genuine |

### 5.2 Empathy Phrases by Situation

**Client waiting for a withdrawal:**
- "I understand the wait causes anxiety. Your withdrawal is in process and I'll confirm the status in [time]."

**Client with unacknowledged deposit:**
- "I know how important it is for your funds to reflect quickly. I'm verifying right now."

**Client who had bad execution:**
- "I'm sorry about that experience. Let's review the execution details to understand exactly what happened."

**Client who had consecutive losing trades:**
- "I understand how hard a losing streak is. If there's anything technical you'd like us to verify, we're here."

**Client wanting to close their account:**
- "I'm sorry you want to leave. Before processing the closure, is there anything we could do differently? If your decision is firm, I respect that completely and will guide you through the process."

### 5.3 What We Should Never Do

- Minimize losses: "It's only $50, it's not that much"
- Give false hope: "Surely the next trade will go well"
- Blame the market as if it were unfair: "The market was crazy today"
- Suggest depositing more to "recover"
- Show indifference: "It's part of trading, everyone loses"

## 6. Response Structure

### 6.1 Standard Response Format

Every response to a client should follow this structure:

1. **Personalized greeting** — Use client's name
2. **Acknowledgment** — Confirm we understand their inquiry or issue
3. **Response/Action** — Provide information or describe what we'll do
4. **Next step** — What's next, who does what, when
5. **Open close** — Offer additional help

**Applied example:**
```
Hi Maria,                                            [1. Greeting]

Thank you for your message about your withdrawal status.  [2. Acknowledgment]

I've verified your request #4521 for $350 USD via 
bank transfer. The withdrawal was approved by 
our team and is in transit to your bank.              [3. Response]

Bank transfer withdrawals take 1-3 business days 
from approval. If you don't see it reflected 
by Friday, write me and I'll trace it with the 
finance team.                                          [4. Next step]

Any other inquiries, I'm here to help.                 [5. Close]
```

### 6.2 Responses Requiring Investigation

If you can't respond immediately:

```
Hi [name],

I received your inquiry about [topic]. I need to verify some details 
with the [corresponding area] team.

I'll confirm a response within the next [realistic time]. 
In the meantime, if you have additional information that could help 
(account number, receipts, screenshots), please send them here.
```

**Rule:** Never leave an inquiry without at least an acknowledgment within the first-response SLA.

## 7. Common Communication Mistakes

### 7.1 Mistakes That Destroy Trust

| Mistake | Impact | Fix |
|-------|---------|------------|
| Copying a predefined response without personalizing it | The client feels they're a number | Always personalize: name, context, data |
| Not following up on a promised case | Breaks trust directly | Set an alarm for the promised follow-up |
| Contradicting what another agent said | Generates confusion and distrust | Read internal notes before responding |
| Promising times that can't be met | Generates disappointment when not met | Give realistic ranges, never aggressive exact times |
| Responding with a curt "no" | Sounds like rejection | Explain why and offer alternative |
| Using internal jargon with the client | The client doesn't understand | Speak in terms anyone would understand |
| Ignoring the emotion of the message | The client feels they don't matter | Acknowledge the emotion before solving the problem |

### 7.2 Broker-Specific Mistakes

| Mistake | Consequence | Fix |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| Saying "your money is 100% safe" | Violates compliance | Say "your funds are in segregated accounts" |
| Suggesting more deposits | Can be seen as undue pressure | Let the client decide how much to deposit |
| Minimizing a bad execution | The client loses trust in the platform | Take it seriously, document, escalate to Dealing |
| Promising exact withdrawal times | If not met, it's worse | Give ranges: "1-3 business days" |
| Speaking evasively about regulation | Generates suspicion | Be direct: "Our license is L15968/N from AOFA" |

## 8. Multichannel Consistency

### 8.1 Main Rule

> The client should receive the same quality of response, the same tone, and the same information regardless of whether they contact us via Intercom, WhatsApp, email, or phone.

### 8.2 Consistency Map

| Element | Intercom | WhatsApp | Email | Phone |
|----------|----------|----------|-------|----------|
| Greeting with name | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Agent identification | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Acknowledgment of issue | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Solution or next step | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes + follow-up email |
| Risk disclaimer | Only if discussing trading | Not necessary | Yes, in signature | Yes, if discussing trading |
| Response time | Playbook SLA | Same SLA as Intercom | Per priority | Immediate (if answered) |
| Post-resolution follow-up | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes, via email |

### 8.3 Channel Handoff

If a client starts on WhatsApp and then writes to Intercom, or vice versa:

1. Search client history across all channels
2. Don't ask for information they already provided on another channel
3. Reference the previous conversation: "I see you spoke with [agent] via [channel] about [topic]"
4. Consolidate the conversation into a single Intercom ticket

## 9. Communication Quality Checklist

### For Self-Review (before sending each response)

- [ ] Did I use the client's name?
- [ ] Did I acknowledge their emotion or situation before giving the response?
- [ ] Is my response clear and leaves no unresolved questions?
- [ ] Did I provide a concrete next step (who, what, when)?
- [ ] Did I avoid any prohibited phrase?
- [ ] Did I personalize the response (not a generic copy-paste)?
- [ ] Is the tone appropriate for the situation?
- [ ] If I promised a time, is it realistic and am I going to keep it?

### For Quality Review (weekly supervisor)

- [ ] Do the responses comply with the 5 fundamental principles?
- [ ] Is the first-response time within SLA?
- [ ] Are follow-ups kept on the promised time?
- [ ] Are predefined responses being adequately personalized?
- [ ] Are there no contradictions between agents on the same case?
- [ ] Do escalated cases have complete internal notes?
