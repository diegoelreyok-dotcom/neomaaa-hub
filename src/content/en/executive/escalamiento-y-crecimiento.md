## 1. The 3 modes of the broker over time

A broker does not operate the same way at month 3 as at year 5. The owner must recognize which mode the company is in and calibrate decisions accordingly. Applying Mode 2 mentality when you are in Mode 1 burns cash without validating; applying Mode 1 when you are in Mode 2 leaves growth on the table.

### 1.1 Mode 1 — Survival (0-12 months)

**Primary objective:** do not go bankrupt.

**Sub-objectives:**
- Impeccable compliance from day 1 (a single material finding in year 1 can cost the new license)
- Positive cashflow or bounded controlled drawdown
- Validated product-market fit (real customers, winning when they win, paying when due)
- Functional operating team (not necessarily complete, but enough for the 3 critical functions: dealing, compliance, support)
- Unit economics trending toward viable LTV:CAC (not necessarily >3 yet, but clear trajectory)

**What gets optimized:**
- Retention > Acquisition (a customer who stays 12 months is worth more than 3 who leave in 3)
- Margin over volume (learn how we win, which flow is profitable, which flow burns)
- Robust financial controls (see `financial-controls.md`)
- Documented processes (basic playbooks for onboarding, withdrawal, hedging, compliance)

**What does NOT get done:**
- DO NOT expand to a new market without validating the main market
- DO NOT add a new product without mastering the core
- DO NOT over-hire (lean team, just-in-time hires)
- DO NOT burn marketing on channels without validated LTV:CAC
- DO NOT pay dividends to principals (all capital reinvested or reserved)

**Key metrics Mode 1:**
- Runway months (target >18 months at all times)
- Client Funds segregation compliance (100%)
- Monthly churn (target <8%)
- NPS (target >30)
- Support response-time rules (99% <24h)

**Typical duration:** 6-18 months depending on initial capital and traction speed.

### 1.2 Mode 2 — Scale (12-36 months)

**Primary objective:** grow aggressively with unit economics discipline.

**Sub-objectives:**
- LTV:CAC >3 sustained 3+ consecutive months → green signal to scale marketing
- EBITDA positive or close (not necessarily profitable yet but clear path)
- Team scale: fill positions that are saturating Mode 1 (Finance Manager, Head of Support, Head of Marketing, Partners Manager)
- Geographic expansion 2-3 secondary markets
- Selective product expansion (MAM/PAMM, social trading if demand is validated)
- Scalable infrastructure (tech + compliance + support scale 3-5x without quality drop)

**What gets optimized:**
- Volume with preserved unit economics (if CAC goes up but LTV also goes up, OK)
- Partners program as acquisition multiplier
- Brand (invest in brand not just performance marketing)
- Team quality (senior hiring, not just filling roles)

**What gets watched:**
- DO NOT expand faster than compliance can scale (each new market = more load on Susana, more KYC, more reporting)
- DO NOT sacrifice margin only for volume (race to zero spread kills the broker)
- DO NOT lose connection with the customer (ticket size goes up, volume goes up, but satisfaction cannot go down)

**Key metrics Mode 2:**
- Monthly revenue growth rate (target 10-25% MoM sustained)
- LTV:CAC (target >3, warning <2.5, critical <2)
- Gross margin (target >50%, warning <40%)
- Headcount productivity (revenue / FTE)
- Open compliance findings (target 0)

**Typical duration:** 18-36 months.

### 1.3 Mode 3 — Optimization (3+ years)

**Primary objective:** maximize profitability, strengthen moats, explore strategic options.

**Sub-objectives:**
- EBITDA margins >25% sustained
- Operational excellence (automated processes, senior team, mature infrastructure)
- Established brand in target niches
- Multiple moats: brand, partners ecosystem, tech stack, regulatory footprint
- Explore: secondary licensing (DFSA, CySEC), strategic acquisitions, partial/total exit, IPO (rare), sustained dividends to principals

**What gets optimized:**
- EBITDA over growth
- Extreme retention (10-year LTV)
- Diversification to reduce dependence (geographic, product, channel)
- Strategic optionality (many routes open)

**Key metrics Mode 3:**
- EBITDA margin
- Revenue per customer (trending up)
- Repeat deposit rate
- Enterprise value / estimated valuation
- ROE (return on equity) to principals

**Duration:** indefinite. Some brokers stay in Mode 3 for 10+ years. Others exit via sale.

## 2. Hiring triggers by role — when to hire what

Hiring is the owner's most critical recurring economic decision. Premature hiring = cash burn without return. Late hiring = team burnout + bottlenecks + missed opportunities. Triggers must be quantitative, not intuitive.

### 2.1 Finance Manager (or Head of Finance)

**Hiring trigger:**
- >100 monthly transactions (between deposits, withdrawals, vendor payments, payroll, reimbursements)
- Yulia spending >60% of time on finance ops (vs 20% initial)
- Need for cashflow forecasting more sophisticated than a weekly spreadsheet

**Typical timing:** Month 3-6 post-launch.

**Salary range:** $4-8K/month base + performance (variable).

**Profile:** CPA or equivalent, broker/fintech experience, bilingual ES/EN, MT5 backend knowledge, availability for audits and reconciliations.

**Reports to:** Yulia (Principal Operations).

### 2.2 Head of Support

**Hiring trigger:**
- >500 monthly tickets
- 2+ junior support agents do not scale quality
- Response time SLAs starting to fail (>10% tickets >24h)
- NPS dropping >5 points in 2 months
- VIP clients requesting unavailable attention

**Typical timing:** Month 6-12.

**Salary range:** $3-6K/month.

**Profile:** experience managing 5+ agents, Intercom proficiency, scripting SLAs, bilingual ES/EN (+ ideally PT for Brazil, RU for CIS), high pain threshold.

**Reports to:** Angel (CEO) or future COO.

### 2.3 Head of Marketing / CMO

**Hiring trigger:**
- Marketing budget >$30K/month
- 3+ simultaneous active channels (Meta, Google, YouTube, Influencer, Affiliate, SEO)
- Need for coherent brand strategy (not just performance)
- Diego spending >30% time on marketing (should be 10-15%)

**Typical timing:** Month 2-4 (may be pre-launch if senior hire is secured).

**Salary range:** $4-8K/month + variable tied to CAC/LTV.

**Profile:** growth experience in fintech/broker/betting/crypto, data-driven, media buying + brand skills, bilingual ES/EN, ideal LATAM connections.

**Reports to:** Diego.

> [!TIP]
> For NEOMAAA, an early Head of Marketing has high leverage. Earlier than Finance or Support. A senior CMO transforms unit economics from day 1.

### 2.4 CFO (Chief Financial Officer)

**Hiring trigger:**
- Revenue >$1M/month sustained
- Active or planned fundraising (VC, PE, strategic)
- M&A on horizon (buy or sell)
- Multi-entity financial structure getting complex (Neomaaa Ltd + Neomaaa UAE + potential Neomaaa EU)

**Typical timing:** Year 2+.

**Salary range:** $8-20K/month + equity.

**Profile:** ex-Big 4 or ex-CFO at established broker, IPO/exit experience ideally, strategic finance not just accounting.

**Reports to:** Diego (CEO).

### 2.5 COO (Chief Operating Officer)

**Hiring trigger:**
- >30 employees
- Multiple departments without central coordination
- Yulia saturated being de facto COO + Operations + Finance
- Need for systemic process optimization

**Typical timing:** Year 1.5-2.

**Salary range:** $7-15K/month + equity.

**Profile:** ex-COO mature broker or ex-operations leader fintech, team builder, process-oriented.

**Reports to:** Diego.

### 2.6 CTO (Chief Technology Officer)

**Hiring trigger:**
- >5 developers in-house or outsourced being managed
- Complex roadmap (custom bridge, proprietary copy trading, mobile app, multiple integrations)
- Material security/tech risk without clear ownership
- Angel saturated doing CEO + interim CTO

**Typical timing:** Year 1-2.

**Salary range:** $6-15K/month + equity.

**Profile:** MT5 stack experience, fintech scale, security/compliance tech, senior architecture.

**Reports to:** Angel (CEO).

### 2.7 Junior Compliance

**Hiring trigger:**
- >500 KYC approvals/month
- Susana >70% time on operational KYC (vs strategic compliance)
- SAR/STR filings >5/month
- Monthly regulatory reporting consuming 3+ days/month

**Typical timing:** Month 6-12.

**Salary range:** $2-4K/month.

**Profile:** KYC/AML experience at broker or bank, CAMS certification preferable, bilingual ES/EN.

**Reports to:** Susana.

### 2.8 Other key roles

**Partners Manager:**
- Trigger: >100 active affiliates or partners with >$500K/mo attributable volume
- When: Month 3-6
- Salary: $3-5K + variable

**Risk Analyst:**
- Trigger: B-Book exposure >$5M + Pepe saturated analyzing flow
- When: Year 2
- Salary: $4-7K

**HR Manager:**
- Trigger: >20 employees + need for formalized people ops
- When: Year 2-3
- Salary: $3-6K

**Institutional Sales / B2B:**
- Trigger: explore IB/white-label/institutional business
- When: Year 2-3
- Salary: $4-8K + high commission

## 3. Geographic expansion triggers

Expanding to a new market is a Board decision, not operational. Real costs: content localization, initial marketing budget $30-100K, local or regional team (at least 1-2 native support + marketing), compliance review, legal review, local payment methods.

### 3.1 Criteria to open a new market

Before opening a market, all 6 criteria must be green:

1. **Retention in current market validated:** monthly churn <8%, NPS >40, repeat deposit rate >50%. If the current market leaks customers, do not expand.

2. **LTV:CAC >3 sustained 3 consecutive months** in core market. If not, Mode 1 is still active.

3. **Team capacity in the new market's language:** minimum 1 support + 1 marketing native or fluent. Without this, expansion fails at CX.

4. **PSPs available and viable in the new market:** local deposits (Pix in Brazil, OXXO Mexico, SPEI, CBU Argentina, cards) with acceptable costs. No payment rails → no market.

5. **Country NOT regulatorily restricted:** not on AOFA prohibited list, not sanctioned, jurisdiction where the Anjouan license allows operation (specific compliance DD).

6. **Marketing budget assigned:** minimum $30K for the first quarter + minimum 6-month investment plan before evaluating results.

### 3.2 Suggested NEOMAAA expansion order

Based on internal capacity (LATAM team + native Spanish), PSPs, opportunity, and progressive diversification:

**1. Brazil (launch market)**
- Language: PT-BR (content pipeline already in motion)
- Key PSP: Pix + cards
- Opportunity: huge, top 5 global retail forex/CFDs market
- Team: needs minimum 1-2 natives (support + marketing)
- Regulatory: Brazil allows offshore broker with disclosure, does not require CVM license

**2. Mexico (month 3-6 post-launch)**
- Language: ES (covered)
- PSPs: SPEI, OXXO, cards
- Opportunity: second LATAM market by size
- Team: covered with Spanish team
- Regulatory: offshore OK, careful with CNBV disclosures

**3. Argentina + Colombia + Chile (month 6-12)**
- Language: ES
- Argentina: limited PSPs (CBU, crypto crucial), huge trader market
- Colombia: PSE, crypto, fast growing
- Chile: WebPay, cards, mature small market
- Team: covered Spanish team
- Regulatory: depends on country; Argentina and Colombia OK offshore, Chile requires careful disclosure

**4. Peru + Ecuador (year 1)**
- Language: ES
- PSPs: variable
- Smaller but less competitive markets
- Gradual onboarding

**5. CIS / Russia (year 1 with careful screening)**
- Language: RU (content pipeline already active)
- PSPs: complex post-sanctions, crypto key
- Opportunity: giant retail forex market but complicated compliance
- Requires: strict OFAC/EU screening, SoF enforcement, exclusion of sanctioned customers
- Decision point Board: is the risk worth the opportunity?

**6. MENA (year 1-2)**
- Language: AR + EN (local hiring needed)
- PSPs: good in UAE, KSA, variable rest
- Opportunity: high average ticket, great appetite for trading
- Requires: potentially DFSA or SCA secondary licensing for legitimacy
- Team: needs MENA native

**7. Africa (year 2)**
- Language: varies (EN Nigeria/Ghana/Kenya, FR West)
- PSPs: mobile money crucial
- Opportunity: emerging markets high growth
- Requires: local understanding, local partners

**8. Asia phase 2 (year 2+)**
- Target countries: Vietnam, Thailand, Indonesia, Malaysia, Philippines
- Language: requires natives (TH, VI, ID, ZH, etc.)
- PSPs: country-specific
- Regulatory: complex country-by-country

## 4. Product expansion triggers

Adding a new product has costs similar to expanding a market: dev time, compliance review, support training, marketing, risk management review. Not every product that can be added should be added.

### 4.1 Criteria to add a product

Before adding a product, all 5 criteria must validate:

1. **Demand confirmed:** >30 customers have explicitly asked (support tickets, surveys, email feedback). If 3 customers ask, it's noise; 30+ is signal.

2. **Compliance approved:** Susana review + legal review. Some products (crypto futures, binary options, high-leverage exotic pairs) have serious regulatory implications.

3. **Dev capacity available:** the dev team can add without sacrificing other critical projects.

4. **Business case revenue > cost:** financial model estimating year 1 expected revenue > total cost (dev + marketing + compliance + incremental support).

5. **Does not distract from core:** the product complements, does not deviate. If the CEO starts spending 50% time on new product and 0% on core, red flag.

### 4.2 Typical product roadmap for retail forex broker

**Base (day 1 — NEOMAAA already has):**
- 4 account types (Standard, Pro, Raw, Demo typically)
- MT5 as primary platform
- 2000+ instruments (FX, metals, indices, commodities, stock CFDs, crypto)
- Diverse leverage (1:30 to 1:500 depending on account)

**Phase 2 (month 3-6):**
- **Copy Trading via MQL5 Signals** (NEOMAAA already has on roadmap): customers subscribe to signals from other traders, MetaTrader manages it. Low implementation cost, high value for retail.
- **Free VPS** for premium customers (retention)

**Phase 3 (month 6-12):**
- **MAM/PAMM accounts:** multiple account management by money manager. Requires: tech platform (MT5 has the plugin), compliance review (money manager as regulated party or as introducer), specific agreements. High value to capture larger funding.
- **MetaTrader 4 coexistence** if there is demand (many old traders prefer MT4, some brokers still offer it alongside MT5).

**Phase 4 (year 1-2):**
- **Proprietary social trading platform** (alternative to MT5 Signals, more UI/UX control): eToro-like. Requires serious dev + complicated compliance.
- **Proprietary branded mobile app** (vs only MT5 mobile): extra branding, custom features.
- **API for algo traders:** REST + FIX for customers with complex EAs.

**Phase 5 (year 2+):**
- **White-label / IB technology:** offer the NEOMAAA stack to third parties (other small brokers, IB networks). B2B model parallel to B2C.
- **Institutional services:** accounts for small/medium funds, hedge funds, family offices.

**Phase 6 (year 3+):**
- **Integrated crypto exchange:** spot trading (not just CFDs), crypto on-ramp/off-ramp. Very complex regulatory.
- **P2P crypto:** matching between customers for crypto deposits/withdrawals.
- **Staking / earn products:** yield on balance (very complex in regulated broker).

> [!WARNING]
> Each phase jump multiplies operational complexity. Do not skip phases. A broker that jumps from basic MT5 to crypto exchange without consolidating Phase 2-3-4 ends up with broken product and pissed-off clients.

## 5. Board KPIs — structured monthly review

The principals' Board must see the same 20 KPIs every month, same definitions, same trends. Do not rotate KPIs every quarter (gives false sense of control). Use 4 categories with 4-5 KPIs each.

### 5.1 Financial KPIs (5)

| KPI | Definition | Target |
|-----|-----------|--------|
| Monthly Revenue | Total revenue month | Growing 10-25% MoM year 1-2 |
| EBITDA | Earnings before interest, tax, depreciation, amortization | Positive from month 12, >20% margin year 2 |
| Cash position | Cash + Reserves available | Minimum 6 months OpEx |
| Runway months | Cash / monthly burn | >18 months always |
| LTV:CAC ratio | Lifetime value / Customer acquisition cost | >3 sustained |
| Revenue per customer | Revenue / active customers month | Trending up |

### 5.2 Operational KPIs (5)

| KPI | Definition | Target |
|-----|-----------|--------|
| FTDs (First-Time Deposits) | New customers with first deposit | Growing monthly target |
| MAU | Monthly active users (traded >0 trades in month) | >70% of total base |
| Monthly churn | % customers stopping deposits | <8% |
| Support tickets | # total + avg response time | <24h avg, 99% <48h |
| NPS | Net Promoter Score | >40 |
| Open compliance findings | # findings pending from any audit | 0 material findings |

### 5.3 Team KPIs (4)

| KPI | Definition | Target |
|-----|-----------|--------|
| Total headcount | # full-time equivalent employees | Per hiring plan |
| Open positions | Unfilled open roles | Minimize time-to-fill <60d |
| Attrition rate | % team leaving company in year | <15% year |
| Employee NPS | Internal satisfaction | >30 |

### 5.4 Market KPIs (4)

| KPI | Definition | Target |
|-----|-----------|--------|
| Share of voice | % positive mentions vs competitors | Trending up |
| Brand awareness | % of LATAM traders who know the brand (survey) | Trending up |
| Web traffic | Monthly sessions | Growing |
| FTD mix by geo | FTD distribution by country | No more than 40% from one country |

### 5.5 Monthly Board meeting — standard agenda

- 5min: personal principals check-in
- 15min: KPI dashboard, any red triggers a discussion point
- 20min: updates by area (Commercial / Operations / Tech / Compliance)
- 30min: decisions of the month (see section 11)
- 15min: risks update (see `risk-management-owner.md`)
- 15min: strategy / look-ahead next quarter
- Close: actions with owners and deadlines

Total: 2 hours maximum. If it extends, preparation problem.

## 6. The 7 most common owner mistakes — patterns to avoid

Industry history: same mistakes repeated. This framework is to not fall into them.

### 6.1 Hiring too fast (payroll bloat)

**Symptom:** headcount grows faster than revenue. Payroll eats 40-60% of revenue instead of 25-35%.

**Typical cause:** owner with capital (own or investor) hires "just in case" or "to have a team that grows with the business". Each role seems necessary in isolation.

**Consequence:** cash burn accelerates, runway compresses, when the market turns there is no flexibility.

**Antidote:** hire only with demonstrated quantitative trigger. "If X metric hits Y threshold, we hire Z". Not before.

### 6.2 Hiring too slow (team burnout)

**Symptom:** existing team working 60+ hrs/week, tickets escalating, founders doing operational work.

**Typical cause:** owner trying to "optimize" payroll or expecting the current team to scale infinitely.

**Consequence:** burnout → rotation → knowledge loss → service crisis → customers leave → negative spiral.

**Antidote:** balance between 6.1 and 6.2 with clear triggers. If trigger hit, hire. Do not wait "one more month".

### 6.3 Scaling marketing prematurely

**Symptom:** burning $50K+/month on Meta/Google before having validated LTV:CAC.

**Typical cause:** FOMO, competitor throwing high budget, growth team wanting to "show numbers".

**Consequence:** cash burn without proportional revenue. It fills with customers that do not retain (scaled channels attract lower-quality). LTV:CAC drops <2.

**Antidote:** validate LTV:CAC >3 with small budget ($5-15K/mo) for 3 months BEFORE scaling. Scale 2x month over month, not 10x in one jump.

### 6.4 Ignoring compliance (the most expensive mistake)

**Symptom:** "we'll fix it later", findings remain open, KYC gets relaxed, Susana has no time or resources.

**Typical cause:** compliance perceived as cost, not infrastructure. Conflict with growth grows.

**Consequence:** an audit detects and it is terminal. License revoked + fine + reputation + personally exposed principals.

**Antidote:** compliance is non-negotiable. Susana reports directly to Board. Open findings are absolute priority. Compliance budget grows with the business.

### 6.5 Over-optimize one market (concentration risk)

**Symptom:** 70% of revenue comes from 1 country.

**Typical cause:** the main market is going well, owner doubles down and pauses expansion.

**Consequence:** local regulatory change, devaluation, political event, PSP crisis in that country → revenue collapses 50% in one month.

**Antidote:** even if Mexico (example) works great, do not let it exceed 35-40% of revenue. Diversify proactively.

### 6.6 Expand without capacity (bad CX, reputation damage)

**Symptom:** launch in Brazil without native PT support, without localization, without local PSP in production → frustrated customer, negative Trustpilot.

**Typical cause:** anxiety for growth, "let's go and adjust along the way".

**Consequence:** in new markets the first impression is the lasting one. A viral thread "NEOMAAA is scam in Brazil" kills the market for years.

**Antidote:** pre-launch checklist per market (section 3.3). Do not open a market without complete check.

### 6.7 Ignoring competitors (irrelevance)

**Symptom:** owner convinced that NEOMAAA is unique. Does not monitor competitors. Does not respond to market changes.

**Typical cause:** arrogance + lack of structured competitive intelligence.

**Consequence:** competitor launches better product, more aggressive pricing, or strategic partnership that takes 20% of the market.

**Antidote:** formal monthly competitive monitoring. At least 5-8 key competitors reviewed. Reports to Board. Response plan when competitor makes material move.

> [!TIP]
> Of the 7 mistakes, the owner typically falls into 2-3 simultaneously. Honest monthly audit: "did I fall into any this month?" If yes, explicit correction plan.

## 7. Capital allocation matrix — what every $100 of EBITDA does

For every $100 of EBITDA generated, the owner decides how to allocate between 6 categories. The correct mix depends on mode (Survival vs Scale vs Optimization) but there are reasonable defaults.

### 7.1 Default allocation matrix (Mode 2 Scale)

| Category | % EBITDA | Objective |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| Marketing reinvestment | 30-40% | Growth in acquisition |
| Hiring | 20-30% | Team scaling |
| Tech infrastructure | 10-15% | Stack robustness + new features |
| Reserves | 15-20% | Safety margin + optionality |
| Compliance / Legal | 5-10% | Audits, legal retainer, training, secondary license exploration |
| Dividends to principals | 0-20% | Limited pre-scale |

**Total: 100%.**

### 7.2 How it varies by mode

**Mode 1 Survival:** Reserves 40-50%, Marketing 20-30%, Team 15-20%, Tech 5-10%, Compliance 10-15%, Dividends 0%.

**Mode 2 Scale:** as table above.

**Mode 3 Optimization:** Reserves 15-20%, Marketing 25-30%, Team 15-20%, Tech 10-15%, Compliance 5-10%, Dividends 20-30%.

### 7.3 Golden rule

**Pre-$1M monthly revenue:** reinvest everything except symbolic dividends ($5-15K/month distributed among principals for tax treatment and recognition). All energy on compounding.

**Post-$1M monthly revenue and positive EBITDA 6+ months:** consider regular dividends agreed by Board (see `treasury-management.md` Overflow section).

### 7.4 Non-negotiable allocation rules

- **Never reduce Reserves below 6 months OpEx.** If growth must be sacrificed to maintain reserves, it is sacrificed.
- **Never reduce Compliance below the minimum framework.** Legal/regulatory savings are penny-wise pound-foolish.
- **Dividends only if all other categories are fully funded for the next quarter.**
- **Tech infrastructure is not allowed to decay.** The temptation to "save on AWS this month" ends in an uptime issue in 6 months.

## 8. Exit and M&A — when to consider

Exit is neither defeat nor the automatic "happy ending". It is a strategic option evaluated when conditions materialize. Thinking exit from day 1 is a mistake; ignoring the exit option at 3-5 years also is.

### 8.1 Triggers to consider exit

- **Attractive valuation:** fintech/broker market multiples rising (recent cycles: 2019-2021 PE crypto/fintech booming; 2023-2024 adjustment).
- **Owners want liquidity:** one of the principals wants to cash out (for personal, strategic, or patrimony diversification reasons).
- **Strategic buyer interested:** a larger broker wants to buy user base, licensing, or team. Proactive buyer deals are rare but happen.
- **Favorable macro:** M&A cycles in the industry. 2017-2018 heavy consolidation; 2021-2022 high multiples; 2024+ recovering.
- **Detected plateau:** the business stops growing significantly (<10% YoY) and owner has no appetite for reinvention. Better to sell than decay.

### 8.2 Exit types

**1. Strategic acquisition:** a larger broker buys for users, tech, brand, license. Typical multiples 2-4x revenue, 5-10x EBITDA. Post-deal integration typically absorbs the acquired brand.

**2. PE (Private Equity) acquisition:** fund buys for cashflow + future option to re-sell. Similar multiples. Management usually stays with offered equity.

**3. Merger:** two brokers combine. No significant cash payout but equity in larger combined entity. Rare in this industry.

**4. IPO (Initial Public Offering):** stock market listing. Extremely rare in retail brokers NEOMAAA's size (need $500M+ minimum revenue). Plenish (Plus500), CMC Markets, IG Group were IPO exits but are exceptions.

**5. Partial sale (secondary):** sell 20-40% of equity without losing control. Liquidity for principals + capital for scale.

### 8.3 Process timeline

- **Preparation (6-12 months):**
  - Financials cleanup (full audit, normalizations, adjustments)
  - Complete compliance without open findings
  - Operational process documentation (the buyer needs to see a systematized business)
  - Team retention plan (golden handcuffs for keys)
  - Pitch deck, CIM (Confidential Information Memorandum)
  - Hire M&A advisor (Ernst & Young Deals, IntelliBroker, specialized boutiques)

- **Process (6-9 months):**
  - Outreach to 15-30 potential buyers
  - NDA with interested parties
  - First round of offers (non-binding)
  - Deep DD with shortlisted 3-5 buyers
  - Second round (binding offers)
  - Final negotiation + SPA (Share Purchase Agreement)

- **Close (3 months):**
  - SPA signing
  - Regulatory approval (AOFA, potentially other jurisdictions)
  - Closing mechanics
  - Cash transfer + escrow setup

**Total: 15-24 months from "let's sell" to cash in bank.**

### 8.4 Valuation — what realistic multiples

Offshore retail broker (NEOMAAA type):

- **Revenue multiple:** 2-4x annual revenue
- **EBITDA multiple:** 5-10x annual EBITDA
- **Per active customer:** $500-2000 USD per active (depositing) customer
- **License premium:** offshore license alone is worth $500K-2M. CySEC/FCA license is worth $5-20M extra.
- **Geographic premium:** LATAM presence has premium if buyer is expansionist European. UAE presence has tax/regional hub premium.
- **Team premium:** experienced team + likely retention = premium ~10-20%.

**Hypothetical NEOMAAA valuation example at year 2:**
- Revenue: $12M annual
- EBITDA: $3M annual
- 15K active clients
- Anjouan license only
- Range: $15-30M total valuation (depending on buyer and conditions)

### 8.5 What NOT to do in exit

- **Do not negotiate alone.** Without M&A advisor the transaction becomes asymmetric (the buyer has a squad, the seller is alone).
- **Do not reveal information pre-NDA.** Diligence-creep can leak data to competitors.
- **Do not agree to crazy earnouts.** Seller commits to hitting post-close targets → all transaction advantage depends on buyer cooperation post-close.
- **Do not underestimate the emotional/personal impact.** Selling what you built is a heavy event. Plan the personal transition (what do you do the day after?).

## 12. Closing for the owner

Sustainable scaling is not linear. It is plateau-climb-plateau. The plateaus are where you consolidate (process, team, product). The climbs are where you invest (hiring, marketing, expansion).

The owner who understands when they are on plateau vs climb makes better decisions. The one who confuses both (invests in climb when they should consolidate, or consolidates when they should climb) loses rhythm.

For Diego, Angel, Yulia and Stanislav: this framework gives you the shared grammar. When one says "we're in Mode 2 with tension toward 3", the others understand the context without debate. When they say "CTO hiring trigger hit", everyone knows what it means.

The best success metric is not revenue at 3 years. It is compound rate at 10 years. The broker that reaches year 10 with $50M revenue growing 15% and 30% EBITDA is worth more (and more enjoyable to operate) than one that hit $100M in year 4 and exploded in year 6.

Play the long game. Scale without breaking. Compounding.
